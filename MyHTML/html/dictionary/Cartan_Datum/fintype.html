Cartan Data of Finite Type

Definition

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $a_{i,j} := \frac{2 i \cdot j}{i \cdot i}$ for each $i,j \in I$.
The Cartan datum $I$ is said to be of finite type if the associated generalized Cartan matrix $(a_{i,j})_{i,j \in I}$ is positive definite ([Lus10, Section 2.1.3]).

Characterization

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
Then, the following are equivalent.
  • $I$ is of finite type.
  • The associated generalized Cartan matrix $(a_{i,j})_{i,j \in I}$ is of finite type.
  • The Weyl group of $I$ is finite.

Properties

  • Each root datum over $(I, \cdot)$ is regular.
