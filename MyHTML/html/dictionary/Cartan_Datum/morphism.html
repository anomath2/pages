Morphisms of Cartan Data

Definition

Let
  • $I,J$: two Cartan data.
A morphism from $I$ to $J$ is a map \[ f: I \rightarrow J \]such that \[ f(i) \cdot f(j) = i \cdot j \text{ for all } i,j \in I. \]
A morphism is said to be an isomorphism if the underlying map is bijective.
A morphism $f : I \rightarrow I$ is said to be an automorphism if it is an isomorphism.

Subdatum

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $J \subset I$: a subset.
Then, one can equip the subset $J$ with a Cartan datum structure by restricting the symmetric bilinear form $\cdot$ on $I$ to $J$. Such a Cartan datum is called a subdatum of $I$.
The inclusion map $J \rightarrow I$ gives rise to a morphism of Cartan data.

Properties

  • If $f: I \rightarrow J$ is a morphism of Cartan data, then the underlying map is injective.
  • A morphism $f: I \rightarrow J$ of Cartan data induces a morphism of the associated generalized Cartan matrices.
