Preface

  • According to Drinfeld, the quantum groups are the Hopf algebras, e.g.,
    • the algebra of regular functions on an algebraic group.
    • the enveloping algebra of a semisimple Lie algebra.
    • the quantized enveloping algebra $\mathbf{U}$.
  • The algebra $\mathbf{U}$ appeared in connection with problems in statistical mechanics, and are closely related to
    • conformal field theory.
    • knot theory.
    • Lie theory.
  • Semisimple Lie groups/algebras are the most important and interesting Lie groups/algebras.
    • They are classified by E. Cartan and Killing around 1890.
    • They are central in today's mathematics.
  • Chevalley in 1950s showed that the semisimple Lie groups can be defined over an arbitrary field, and even over integers.
  • The algebra $\mathbf{U}$ has a natural $\mathbb{Z}[v,v^{-1}]$-form.
    • It is specialized to the Kostant $\mathbb{Z}$-form of the enveloping algebra at $v = 1$.
    • It is specialized at $v = $ root of $1$, giving some new objects which include quantum versions of the semisimple groups over fields of positive characteristic.
