Lemma 15.1.1

The algebra homomorphism \[ \chi: {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f} \rightarrow \mathbf{U} \]given by \[ \chi(\theta_i) := E'_i := (v_i-v_i^{-1})K_i^{-1} E_i \text{ for all } i \in I \]factors through an algebra homomorphism $\mathbf{f} \rightarrow \mathbf{U}$.

15.1.2

Set \[ \tilde{\mathbf{U}}^+ := \chi({}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}). \]Let $\tilde{\mathbf{U}}^0$ denote the subalgebra of $\mathbf{U}$ generated by $K_i^{-1}$, $i \in i$. Let $\tilde{\mathbf{U}}$ denote the subalgebra of $\mathbf{U}$ generated by $\mathbf{U}^-$, $\tilde{\mathbf{U}}^0$, and $\tilde{\mathbf{U}}^+$. Let $\mathfrak{A}$ denote the quotient algebra of $\tilde{\mathbf{U}}$ factored by the two-sided ideal generated by $K_i^{-1}$, $i \in I$.
  • (a) The multiplication map defined isomorphisms of vector spaces \[ \mathbf{U}^- \otimes \tilde{\mathbf{U}}^+ \simeq \mathfrak{A}, \quad \tilde{\mathbf{U}}^+ \otimes \mathbf{U}^- \simeq \mathfrak{A}. \]
  • (b) $\mathfrak{A}$ is isomorphic to the algebra with generators \[ \{ \epsilon_i, \phi_i \mid i \in I \} \] subject to the following relations: \begin{align*} \begin{split} &\epsilon_i \phi_j = v^{i \cdot j} \phi_j \epsilon_i + \delta_{i,j}, \\ &f(\epsilon_i \mid i \in I) = f(\phi_i \mid i \in I) = 0 \text{ for all } f(\theta_i \mid i \in I) \in \mathcal{I}. \end{split} \end{align*} An isomorphism is given by \[ E'_i \mapsto \epsilon_i, \quad F_i \mapsto \phi_i. \]

Lemma 15.1.4

  • $\mathbf{f}$ is a left $\mathfrak{A}$-module by \[ \phi_i = \text{the left multiplication by $\theta_i$}, \quad \epsilon_i = {}_i r. \]
  • $\epsilon_i$ acts on $\mathbf{f}$ locally nilpotently.

Lemma 15.1.5

There exists a unique algebra homomorphism \[ d : \mathfrak{A} \rightarrow \mathfrak{A} \otimes \mathbf{U} \]such that \[ d(\phi_i) = \phi_i \otimes K_i^{-1} + 1 \otimes F_i, \quad d(e_i) = e_i \otimes K_i^{-1} + (v_i-v_i^{-1}) \otimes K_i^{-1} E_i. \]
