17.3. The Operators $\tilde{\phi}_i, \tilde{\epsilon}_i: \mathbf{f} \rightarrow \mathbf{f}$

17.3.1

We shall regard $\mathbf{f}$ as an $\mathfrak{A}$-module. Then, for each $i \in I$, we have $\mathbf{f} \in \mathcal{D}_i$. Note that the inner product on $\mathbf{f}$ is admissible.

17.3.3

Set \[ \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{f}) := \{ x \in {}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f} \mid (x,x) \in \mathbf{f} \}. \]

Corollary 17.3.7

Let $i \in I$ and $b \in \mathcal{B}_{i;t}$. Let $b_0 \in \mathcal{B}_{i;0}$ be the unique element such that $\pi_{i,t}(b_0) = b$. Then, we have
  • (a) $\tilde{\phi}_i b = \pi_{i,t+1}(b_0) \pmod{v^{-1} \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{f})}$.
  • (b) $\tilde{\epsilon}_i b = \pi_{i,t-1}(b_0) \pmod{v^{-1} \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{f})}$ if $t \geq 1$, and $\tilde{\epsilon}_i b \in v^{-1} \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{f})$ if $t = 0$.
  • (c) If $i \in I$ and $b \in \mathcal{B}$, then $\tilde{\phi}_i b = b' \pmod{v^{-1} \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{f})}$ for a unique $b' \in \mathcal{B}$. Moreover, $\tilde{\epsilon}_i b' = b \pmod{v^{-1} \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{f})}$.
  • (d) If $i \in I$ and $b \in \mathcal{B}_{i;n}$ for some $n > 0$, then $\tilde{\epsilon}_i b = b'' \pmod{v^{-1} \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{f})}$ for a unique $b'' \in \mathcal{B}$. Moreover, $\tilde{\phi}_i b'' = b \pmod{v^{-1} \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{f})}$.
