16.3. Adapted Bases

Assumption

In this chapter, we fix $i \in I$. $P, M, (,)$ are as in the previous section.

16.3.1

We say that a basis $B$ of $P$ is adapted if there exists a partition $B = \bigcup_{n \geq 0} B(n)$ and bijections \[ \pi_n : B(0) \rightarrow B(n) \]such that
  • (a) for each $N \geq 0$, $B(N) \cup B(N+1) \cup \cdots$ is a basis of $\phi_i^{(N)} P$.
  • (b) for each $b \in B(0)$ and $N \geq 0$, we have $\phi_i^{(N)}b - \pi_N(b) \in \phi_i^{(N+1)} P$.
We say that a basis $B$ of $M$ is adapted if there exists a partition $B = \bigcup_{s,t \geq 0, s+t \geq 0} B(s,t)$ and bijections \[ \pi_{s,t} : B(0,2s+t) \rightarrow B(s,t) \]such that
  • (a) $B(s,t) \cup B(s+1,t) \cup B(s+2,t) \cup \cdots$ is a basis of $M^t \cap F_i^{(s)} M$.
  • (b) for each $b \in B(2s+t)$, we have $F_i^{(s)}b - \pi_{s,t}(b) \in F_i^{(s+1)} M$.
In this section, it is assumed that $B$ is integral, almost orthonormal, and adapted.
