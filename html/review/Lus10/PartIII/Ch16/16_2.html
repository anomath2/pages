16.2. Admissible Forms

Assumption

In this chapter, we fix $i \in I$.

16.1.1

Fix \[ P \in \mathcal{D}_i, \quad M \in \mathcal{C}'_i. \]

16.1.2

A symmetric bilinear form $(,)$ on $P$ is said to be admissible if \[ (x, \epsilon_i y) = (1-v_i^{-2})(\phi_i x, y) \text{ for all } x,y \in P. \]
A symmetric bilinear form $(,)$ on $M$ is said to be admissible if
  • $(M^n, M^m) = 0$ for all $n \neq m$.
  • $(E_i x, y) = v_i^{n-1}(x, F_i y)$ for all $x \in M^{n-2}$, $y \in M^n$.

16.2.4

Let $B$ be a basis of $P$ (resp., $M$). We say that $B$ is integral if the $\mathcal{A}$-submodule ${}_\mathcal{A} P$ of $P$ generated by $B$ is stable under $\epsilon_i, \phi_i^(t)$ (resp., the $\hat{\mathcal{A}}$-submodule ${}_{\hat{\mathcal{A}}} M$ of $M$ generated by $B$ is stable under $E_i^{(t)}, F_i^{(t)}$).
Assume that we are given an admissible form and an integral basis $B$ which is almost orthonormal. Set \[ \mathcal{L}(P) := \{ x \in {}_\mathcal{A} P \mid (x,x) \in \mathbf{A} \}, \quad \mathcal{L}(M) := \{ x \in {}_{\hat{\mathcal{A}}} M \mid (x,x) \in \mathbf{A} \}. \]

Lemma 16.2.5

  • (a) $\mathcal{L}(P)$ is a $\mathbb{Z}[v^{-1}]$-submodule of ${}_\mathcal{A} P$ and $B$ is a basis of it.
  • (b) Let $x \in {}_\mathcal{A} P$ be such that $(x,x) \in 1 + v^{-1} \mathbf{A}$. Then, there exists $b \in B$ such that $x = \pm b \pmod{v^{-1} \mathcal{L}(P)}$.
  • (c) Let $x \in {}_\mathcal{A} P$ be such that $(x,x) \in v^{-1} \mathbf{A}$. Then, $x \in v^{-1} \mathcal{L}(P)$.
  • (d) $\mathcal{L}(M)$ is a $\mathbf{A}(\mathbb{Z})$-submodule of ${}_{\hat{\mathcal{A}}} M$ and $B$ is a basis of it.
  • (e) Let $x \in {}_{\hat{\mathcal{A}}} M$ be such that $(x,x) \in 1 + v^{-1} \mathbf{A}$. Then, there exists $b \in B$ such that $x = \pm b \pmod{v^{-1} \mathcal{L}(M)}$.
  • (c) Let $x \in {}_{\hat{\mathcal{A}}} M$ be such that $(x,x) \in v^{-1} \mathbf{A}$. Then, $x \in v^{-1} \mathcal{L}(M)$.

Lemma 16.2.8

  • (a) $\tilde{\phi}_i$, $\tilde{\epsilon}_i$ map $\mathcal{L}(P)$ into itself.
  • (b) $\tilde{F}_i$, $\tilde{E}_i$ map $\mathcal{L}(M)$ into itself.
