Abstract

  • In the author's elementary algebraic definition [Lus90] of the canonical basis of $\mathbf{f}$, there were three main ingredients:
    • the basis was assumed to be integral in a suitable sense.
    • the basis was assumed to be fixed by the involution $\overline{\cdot}$.
    • the basis was assumed to be in a specified $\mathbb{Z}[v^{-1}]$-lattice $\mathcal{L}$, and had prescribed image in $\mathcal{L}/v^{-1} \mathcal{L}$.
    The lats one is the most subtle. In [Lus90], the lattice $\mathcal{L}$ and the basis of $\mathcal{L}/v^{-1} \mathcal{L}$ were defined in terms of a braid group action, which does not work for Cartan data of infinite type.
  • Kashiwara proposes a quite different way to construct the lattice $\mathcal{L}$ and the basis of $\mathcal{L}/v^{-1} \mathcal{L}$ in terms of Kashiwara operators, which makes sense for every Cartan datum.
  • The algebra $\mathfrak{A}$ is defined in Chapter 15 in a different way than in [Kas91], bt eventually, the two definitions agree.
  • Kashiwara operators are defined in Chapter 16.
  • Chapter 20 deals with crystal bases.
