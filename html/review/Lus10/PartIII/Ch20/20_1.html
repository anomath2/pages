20.1. The Basis at $\infty$ of $\Lambda_\lambda$

20.1.1

Let $M \in \mathcal{C}'$. A basis at $\infty$ of $M$ is a pair consisting of
  • (a) a free $\mathbf{A}$-submodule $L$ of $M$ such that \[ M = \mathbb{Q}(v) \otimes_{\mathbf{A}} L = M. \]
  • (b) a basis $\mathbf{b}$ of the $\mathbb{Q}$-vector space $L/v^{-1}L$.
satisfying the following.
  • (c) $L$ is stable under the operators $\tilde{E}_i, \tilde{F}_i$.
  • (d) $\tilde{F}_i(\mathbf{b}) \subset \mathbf{b} \sqcup \{0\}$, $\tilde{E}_i(\mathbf{b}) \subset \mathbf{b} \sqcup \{0\}$ for all $i \in I$.
  • (e) $L = \bigoplus_{\lambda \in X} L^\lambda$, where $L^\lambda := M^\lambda \cap L$, and $\mathbf{b} = \bigsqcup_{\lambda \in X} \mathbf{b}^\lambda$, where $\mathbf{b}^\lambda := (L^\lambda/v^{-1} L^\lambda) \cap \mathbf{b}$.
  • (f) For each $b,b' \in \mathbf{b}$ and $i \in I$, we have $\tilde{E}_i b = b'$ if and only if $\tilde{F}_i b' = b$.
The definition above is due to Kashiwara, who calls them crystal bases.

Theorem 20.1.4

Assume that the root datum is $Y$-regular. Let $\lambda \in X^+$. Set $L$ to be the $\mathbf{A}$-submodule of $\Lambda_\lambda$ generated by $\mathbf{B}(\Lambda_\lambda)$ and $\mathbf{b}$ to be the image of $\mathbf{B}(\Lambda_\lambda)$ in $L/v^{-1} L$. Then, $(L, \mathbf{b})$ is a basis at $\infty$ of $\Lambda_\lambda$.
