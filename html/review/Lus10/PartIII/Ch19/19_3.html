19.3. Further Properties of the Inner Product

Assumption

In this chapter, the root datum is $Y$-regular, and $\lambda \in X^+$ is fixed.

19.3.1

Let ${}_\mathcal{A} \Lambda_\lambda$ denote the image of ${}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f} \rightarrow \Lambda_\lambda$.

Proposition 19.3.2

${}_\mathcal{A} \Lambda_\lambda$ is stable under the action of $x^\pm$ for all $x \in {}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f}$.

Proposition 19.3.3

Let $b,b' \in \mathcal{B}(\lambda)$.
  • (a) If $b' \neq \pm b$, then $(b^- \eta_\lambda, (b')^- \eta_\lambda) \in v^{-1} \mathbb{Z}[v^{-1}]$.
  • (b) $(b^- \eta_\lambda, b^- \eta_\lambda) \in 1 + v^{-1} \mathbb{Z}[v^{-1}]$.
  • (c) $({}\mathcal{A} \Lambda_\lambda, {}\mathcal{A} \Lambda_\lambda) \subset \mathcal{A}$.

19.3.4

There exists a unique $\mathbb{Q}$-linear map $\overline{\cdot}$ on $\Lambda_\lambda$ such that \[ \overline{u \eta_\lambda} = \overline{u} \eta_\lambda \]for all $u \in \mathbf{U}$.

Theorem 19.3.5

Let $b \in \Lambda_\lambda$. Then, we have $b \in \mathbf{B}(\Lambda_\lambda)$ if and only if
  • (1) $b \in {}_\mathcal{A} \Lambda_\lambda$ and $\overline{b} = b$.
  • (2) There exists a sequence $i_1,\dots,i_r \in I$ such that \[ b = \tilde{F}_{i_1} \tilde{F}_{i_2} \cdots \tilde{F}_{i_r} \eta_\lambda \pmod{v^{-1} L(\Lambda_\lambda)}. \]
