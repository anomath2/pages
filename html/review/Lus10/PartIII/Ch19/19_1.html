19.1. First Properties of the Inner Product

Assumption

In this chapter, the root datum is $Y$-regular, and $\lambda \in X^+$ is fixed.

19.1.1

Let $\rho$ be the anti-algebra isomorphism on $\mathbf{U}$ such that \[ \rho(E_i) = v_i K_i F_i, \quad \rho(F_i) = v_i K_i^{-1} E_i, \quad \rho(K_h) = K_h. \]We have \[ \rho-^2 = 1. \]

19.1.2

There is a unique bilinear form on $\Lambda_\lambda$ such that
  • (a) $(\eta_\lambda, \eta_\lambda) = 1$.
  • (b) $(ux,y) = (x, \rho(u)y)$ for all $x,y \in \Lambda_\lambda$, $u \in \mathbf{U}$.
Moreover, it is symmetric.

Proposition 19.1.3

Let $\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$.
  • (a) $(L(\Lambda_\lambda), L(\Lambda_\lambda)) \in \mathbf{A}$.
  • (b) For each $i \in I$ with $\nu_i > 0$ and $x \in L(\Lambda_\lambda)_{\nu-i}$, $y \in L(\Lambda_\lambda)_\nu$, we have \[ (\tilde{F}_i x, y) = (x, \tilde{E}_i y) \pmod{v^{-1} \mathbf{A}}. \]
