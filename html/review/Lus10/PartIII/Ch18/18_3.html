18.3. Further Consequences of the General Hypothesis

Assumption

In this chapter, we assume that the root datum is $Y$-regular, and $\lambda \in X^+$ is fixed.

Theorem 18.3.8

Let $\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$.
  • (a) $L(\Lambda_\lambda)$ coincides with the $\mathbf{A}$-submodule generated by the elements \[ \tilde{F}_{i_1} \tilde{F}_{i_2} \cdots \tilde{F}_{i_r} \eta_\lambda \] for various $i_1,\dots,i_r \in I$.
  • (b) For each $i \in I$ and $x \in \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{f})$, we have \[ \tilde{F}_i(x^- \eta_\lambda) = (\tilde{\phi}_i x)^- \eta_\lambda \pmod{v^{-1} L(\Lambda_\lambda)}. \]
  • (c) If $i \in I$ is such that $\nu_i > 0$, then $\tilde{E}_i(b^- \eta_\lambda) = (\tilde{\epsilon}_i b)^- \eta_\lambda \pmod{v^{-1} L(\Lambda_\lambda)}$ for all $b \in \mathcal{B}_\nu$ such that $b^- \eta_\lambda \neq 0$.
