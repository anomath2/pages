Notes

  • The canonical basis of the Lusztig algebra $\mathbf{f}$ was introduced by Lusztig [Lus90] for symmetric finite type in two ways; an algebraic approach based on braid group actions, and a geometric approach based on quivers and perverse sheaves.
  • Arbitrary symmetric case was constructed by Lusztig [Lus91a] in a geometric way.
  • The general case was constructed by Kashiwara [Kas91] in an algebraic way (grand loop argument) and by Lusztig [Lus10] in a geometric way.
  • The coincidence of [Lus90] and [Kas91] for symmetric finite type was proved in [Lus91].
  • The coincidence of [Lus91a] and [Kas91] for symmetric type was proved in [GL93].
