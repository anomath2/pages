14.2. The Signed Basis $\mathcal{B}$

Theorem 14.2.3

Set \[ \mathcal{B} := \{ x \in {}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f} \mid \overline{x} = x \text{ and } (x,x) \in 1 + v^{-1}\mathbb{Z}[\![v^{-1}]\!] \}. \]
  • (a) $\mathcal{B}$ is a signed basis of ${}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f}$.
  • (b) If $b,b' \in \mathcal{B}$ are such that $b' \neq \pm b$, then \[ (b,b') \in v^{-1} \mathbb{Z}[\![v^{-1}]\!]. \]

14.2.4

The set $\mathcal{B}$ is called the canonical signed basis of $\mathbf{f}$.

14.2.5

  • (a) $\mathcal{B} = \bigsqcup_{\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]} \mathcal{B}_\nu$, where $\mathcal{B}_\nu := \mathbf{f}_\nu \cap \mathcal{B}$.
  • (b) $(\theta_i^{(n)}) \in \mathcal{B}$ for all $i \in I$, $n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$.
  • (c) $\sigma(\mathcal{B}) = \mathcal{B}$.
