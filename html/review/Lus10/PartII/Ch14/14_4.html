14.4. The Canonical Basis $\mathbf{B}$ of $\mathbf{f}$

14.4.2

For each $\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$, defined $\mathbf{B}_\nu \subset \mathcal{B}_\nu$ by induction on $\operatorname{tr} \nu$ as follows. If $\nu = 0$, then set \[ \mathbf{B}_0 := \{ 1 \}. \]If $\operatorname{tr} \nu > 0$, then set \[ \mathbf{B}_\nu := \bigcup_{\substack{i \in I \\ 0 < n \leq \nu_i}} \pi_{i,n}(\mathbf{B}_{\nu - ni} \cap \mathcal{B}_{i;0}). \]
Set \[ \mathbf{B} := \bigsqcup_{\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]} \mathbf{B}_\nu. \]

Theorem 14.4.3

Let $\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$.
  • (a) $\mathbf{B}_\nu \cap (-\mathbf{B}_\nu) = \emptyset$.
  • (b) $\mathbf{B}_\nu \cap (-\sigma(\mathbf{B}_\nu)) = \emptyset$.
  • (c) $\sigma(\mathbf{B}_\nu) = \mathbf{B}_\nu$.
  • (d) $\mathbf{B}$ is a basis of ${}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f}$.
  • (e) $\mathbf{B}_\nu$ is a basis of ${}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f}_\nu$.

14.4.6

The set $\mathbf{B}$ is called the canonical basis of $\mathbf{f}$.
Set \[ \mathbf{B}_{i;n} := \mathcal{B}_{i;n} \cap \mathbf{B}, \quad {}^\sigma \mathbf{B}_{i;n} := \sigma(\mathbf{B}_{i;n}). \]

Theorem 14.4.11

Assume that the root datum is $Y$-regular. Let $\lambda \in X^+$. Set \[ \mathbf{B}(\lambda) := \bigcap_{i \in I} \left( \bigcup_{n=0}^{\langle h_i, \lambda \rangle} {}^\sigma \mathbf{B}_{i;n} \right). \]
  • (a) The map $b \mapsto b^- \eta_\lambda$ defines a bijection of $\mathbf{B}(\lambda)$ onto a basis $\mathbf{B}(\Lambda_\lambda)$ of $\Lambda_\lambda$.
  • (b) If $b \in \mathbf{B} \setminus \mathbf{B}(\lambda)$, then $b^- \eta_\lambda = 0$.

14.4.12

The set $\mathbf{B}(\Lambda_\lambda)$ is called the canonical basis of $\Lambda_\lambda$.

Theorem 14.4.13

Assume that the Cartan datum is symmetric.
  • (a) For each $b,b' \in \mathbf{B}$, we have \[ b b' = \sum_{b'' \in \mathbf{B},\ n \in \mathbb{Z}} c_{b,b',b'',n} v^n b'', \] where $c_{b,b',b'',n} \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ are zero except for finitely many $b''$, $n$.
  • (b) For each $b \in \mathbf{B}$, we have \[ r(b) = \sum_{b',b'' \in \mathbf{B},\ n \in \mathbb{Z}} d_{b,b',b'',n} v^n b' \otimes b'', \] where $d_{b,b',b'',n} \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ are zero except for finitely many $b$, $b''$, $n$.
  • (c) For each $b,b' \in \mathbf{B}$, we have \[ (b, b') = \sum_{n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}} f_{b,b',n} v^{-n}, \] where $f_{b,b',n} \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$.
