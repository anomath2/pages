14.3. The Subsets $\mathcal{B}_{i;n}$ of $\mathcal{B}$

14.3.1

For $i \in I$ and $n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$, set \[ \mathcal{B}_{i; \geq n} := \mathcal{B} \cap \theta_i^n \mathbf{f}, \quad {}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i; \geq n} := \mathcal{B} \cap \mathbf{f} \theta_i^n, \]\[ \mathcal{B}_{i;n} := \mathcal{B}_{i, \geq n} \setminus \mathcal{B}_{i, \geq n+1}, \quad {}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i;n} := {}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i, \geq n} \setminus {}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i, \geq n+1}. \]
We have \[ \mathcal{B}_{i; \geq n} = \bigsqcup_{n' \geq n} \mathcal{B}_{i;n'}, \quad {}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i; \geq n} = \bigsqcup_{n' \geq n} {}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i;n'}, \]\[ {}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i; \geq n} = \sigma(\mathcal{B}_{i; \geq n}), \quad {}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i;n} = \sigma(\mathcal{B}_{i;n}). \]

Theorem 14.3.2

  • (a) $\mathcal{B}_{i; \geq n}$ is a signed basis of $\sum_{n' \geq n} \theta_i^{(n)} {}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f}$.
  • (b) ${}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i; \geq n}$ is a signed basis of $\sum_{n' \geq n} {}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f} \theta_i^{(n)}$.
  • (c) For each $b \in \mathcal{B}_{i;0}$, there exists a unique element $b' \in \mathcal{B}_{i;n}$ such that \[ \theta_i^{(n)} b \in b' + \mathcal{A} \mathcal{B}_{i; \geq n+1}. \] Moreover, $b \mapsto b'$ is a bijection $\pi_{i,n} : \mathcal{B}_{i;0} \rightarrow \mathcal{B}_{i;n}$.
  • (d) For each $b \in {}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i;0}$, there exists a unique element $b' \in {}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i;n}$ such that \[ b \theta_i^{(n)} \in b' + \mathcal{A} {}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i; \geq n+1}. \] Moreover, $b \mapsto b'$ is a bijection ${}^\sigma \pi_{i,n} : {}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i;0} \rightarrow {}^\sigma \mathcal{B}_{i;n}$.
