40.2. The Subspace $\mathbf{U}^+(w,e)$ of $\mathbf{U}^+$

Proposition 40.2.1

Let $w \in W$, $e = \pm 1$, $\mathbf{h} = (i_1,\dots,i_n)$ a reduced word for $w$.
  • (b) The elements $E_{i_1}^{(c_i)} T'_{i_1,e}(E_{i_2}^{(c_2)}) \cdots T'_{i_1,e} \cdots T'_{i_{n-1},e}(E_{i_n}^{(c_n)})$ for various $(c_1,\dots,c_n) \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}^n$, form a basis a subspace of $\mathbf{U}^+$, which is independent of $\mathbf{h}$, and is denoted by $\mathbf{U}^+(w,e)$.
  • (c) The elements $E_{i_1}^{(c_i)} T''_{i_1,e}(E_{i_2}^{(c_2)}) \cdots T''_{i_1,e} \cdots T''_{i_{n-1},e}(E_{i_n}^{(c_n)})$ for various $(c_1,\dots,c_n) \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}^n$, form a basis of $\mathbf{U}^+$.
  • (d) If $\ell(s_i w) = \ell(w)-1$, then $E_i \mathbf{U}^+(w,e) \subset \mathbf{U}^+(w,e)$.

Corollary 40.2.2

Assume that the Cartan datum is of finite type. Then, \[ \mathbf{U}^+(w_0, e) = \mathbf{U}^+. \]
