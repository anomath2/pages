33.1. The Specialization $v=1$

33.1.1

Let $R$ be a commutative ring, and $\phi: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow R$ a ring homomorphism.
Let ${}_R \tilde{\mathbf{f}}$ denote the quotient algebra of ${}_R {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ by the two-sided ideal generated by \[ \Phi_{i,j} := \sum_{p+p' = 1-a_{i,j}} (-1)^{p'} \phi({p+p' \brack p}_i) \theta_i^p \theta_j \theta_i^{p'} \]for various $i \neq j \in I$.

Theorem 33.1.3

Assume that the root datum is $X$-regular and $Y$-regular. Let $R_0$ be a field of characteristic $0$, and $\phi: \mathcal{A} \rightarrow R_0$ a ring homomorphism which sends $v$ to $1$.
  • (a) The natural algebra homomorphism ${}_{\mathbb{Q}(v)} \tilde{\mathbf{f}} \rightarrow \mathbf{f}$ is an isomorphism.
  • (b) $\dim_{\mathbb{Q}(v)} \mathbf{f}_\nu = \dim_{R_0} ({}_{R_0} \tilde{\mathbf{f}}_\nu)$ for all $\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$.
  • (c) The natural algebra homomorphism ${}_{R_0} \tilde{\mathbf{f}} \rightarrow {}_{R_0} \mathbf{f}$ is an isomorphism.
  • (d) If $\lambda \in X^+$, then the dimension of the weight spaces of $\Lambda_\lambda$ are the same as those of the simple integrable highest weight representation of the corresponding Kac-Moody algebra.

Corollary 33.1.5

The algebra $\mathbf{U}$ has (a)--(d) in 3.1.1 and the quantum Serre relations as the defining relations.
