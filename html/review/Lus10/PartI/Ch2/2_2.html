2.2. Root Datum

2.2.1

A root datum of type $(I, \cdot)$ is a quintuple $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$ consisting of
  • two finitely generated free abelian groups $Y,X$,
  • a perfect pairing $\langle , \rangle: Y \times X \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}$,
  • two subsets $\Pi^\vee = \{ h_i \mid i \in I \} \subset Y$ and $\{ \alpha_i \mid i \in I \} \subset X$,
satisfying \[ \langle h_i, \alpha_j \rangle = a_{i,j} \text{ for all } i,j \in I. \]
Let \[ h: \mathbb{Z}[I] \rightarrow Y;\ \nu = \sum_{i \in I} \nu_i i \mapsto \sum_{i \in I} \nu_i h_i, \]\[ \alpha: \mathbb{Z}[I] \rightarrow X;\ \nu = \sum_{i \in I} \nu_i i \mapsto \sum_{i \in I} \nu_i \alpha_i. \]

2.2.2

A rood datum $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$ is said to be
  • $X$-regular if $\Pi$ is linearly independent.
  • $Y$-regular if $\Pi^\vee$ is linearly independent.

2.2.3

If the root datum is $X$-regular, then there exists a partial order $\leq$ on $X$ defined as follows. For each $\lambda,\mu \in X$, we have $\lambda \leq \mu$ if and only if $\mu - \lambda \in \alpha(\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I])$.

2.2.6

The Weyl group $W$ acts on $Y$ and $X$ by \[ s_i(h) := h - \langle h, \alpha_i \rangle h_i, \quad s_i(\lambda) := \lambda - \langle h_i, \lambda \rangle \alpha_i \text{ for all } i \in I,\ h \in Y,\ \lambda \in X. \]
We have \[ \langle w(h), w(\lambda) \rangle = \langle h, \lambda \rangle \]for all $w \in W$, $h \in Y$, $\lambda \in X$.

2.2.7

Let $s_{i_1} \cdots s_{i_r}$ be a reduced expression of an element of $W$. Then, \[ s_{i_r} \cdots s_{i_2}(h_{i_1}) \in h(\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]), \quad s_{i_r} \cdots s_{i_2}(\alpha_{i_1}) \in \alpha(\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]). \]
