5.2. First Properties of $T'_{i,e}$, $T''_{i,e}$

5.2.1

In this chapter, we fix $i \in I$.
Fix $e \in \{ 1, -1 \}$.
Let $M \in \mathcal{C}'_i$. Define two endomorphisms $T'_{i,e}$ and $T''_{i,e}$, called symmetries on $M$ by \begin{align*} \begin{split} &T'_{i,e}(m) := \sum_{a-b+c=n} (-1)^b v_i^{e(-ac+b)} F_i^{(a)} E_i^{(b)} F_i^{(c)} m, \\ &T''_{i,e}(m) := \sum_{a-b+c=-n} (-1)^b v_i^{e(-ac+b)} E_i^{(a)} F_i^{(b)} E_i^{(c)} m, \end{split} \end{align*}for all $m \in M^n$.

Proposition 5.2.2

Let $n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ and $0 \leq k \leq n$.
  • (a) If $\eta \in M^n$ is such that $E_i \eta = 0$, then \[ T'_{i,e}(F_i^{(k)} \eta) = (-1)^k v_i^{ek(n-k+1)} F_i^{(n-k)} \eta. \]
  • (b) If $\xi \in M^{-n}$ is such that $F_i \xi = 0$, then \[ T''_{i,e}(E_i^{(k)} \xi) = (-1)^k v_i^{ek(n-k+1)} E_i^{(n-k)} \xi. \]

Proposition 5.2.3

  • (a) $T'_{i,e} T''_{i,-e} = T''_{i,-e} T'_{i,e} = 1$.

Proposition 5.2.7

Let $M \in \mathcal{C}'$ and $\lambda \in X$. Then, \[ T'_{i,e}(M^\lambda) = T''_{i,e}(M^\lambda) = M^{s_i(\lambda)}. \]
