1.4. Quantum Serre Relations

1.4.1

For $n \in \mathbb{Z}$ and $i \in I$, \[ \theta_i^{(n)} := \begin{cases} \frac{\theta_i^n}{[n]_i!} & \text{ if } n \geq 0, \\ 0 & \text{ if } n < 0. \end{cases} \]

Proposition 1.4.3

For each $i \neq j \in I$, we have \[ \sum_{r+s = 1-a_{i,j}} (-1)^s \theta_i^{(r)} \theta_j \theta_i^{(s)} = 0 \text{ in } \mathbf{f}. \]The identities above are called the quantum Serre relations.

1.4.7

Let ${}_\mathcal{A} \mathbf{f}$ denote the $\mathcal{A}$-subalgebra of $\mathbf{f}$ generated by \[ \{ \theta_i^{(n)} \mid i \in I,\ n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0} \}. \]
We have \[ {}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f} = \bigoplus_{\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]} {}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f}_\nu, \quad {}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f}_\nu := \mathbf{f}_\nu \cap {}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f}. \]
