1.1. Cartan Data

1.1.1

A Cartan datum is a pair $(I, \cdot)$ consisting of
  • a finite set $I$,
  • a symmetric bilinear form $\cdot: \mathbb{Z}[I] \times \mathbb{Z}[I] \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}$,
satisfying the following for all $i,j \in I$.
  • $i \cdot i \in 2\mathbb{Z}_{> 0}$.
  • $a_{i,j} := \frac{2 i \cdot j}{i \cdot i} \in \mathbb{Z}_{\leq 0}$ if $i \neq j$.
Two Cartan data $(I, \cdot)$ and $(I, \circ)$ are said to be proportional if there exist $a,b \in \mathbb{Z}_{> 0}$ such that \[ a i \circ j = b i \cdot j \text{ for all } i,j \in I. \]

1.1.2

Let
  • $v$: an indeterminate.
  • $v_i := v^{d_i}$, where $d_i := \frac{i \cdot i}{2}$ for all $i \in I$.
  • For $P \in \mathbb{Q}(v)$, let $P_i$ denote the rational function obtained from $P$ by substituting $v$ by $v_i$.
  • For $\nu = \sum_{i \in I} \nu_i i \in \mathbb{Z}[I]$, set \[ v_\nu := \prod_{i \in I} v_i^{\nu_i}, \quad \operatorname{tr} \nu := \sum_{i \in I} \nu_i. \]
