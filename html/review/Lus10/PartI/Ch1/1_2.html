1.2. The Algebras ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ and $\mathbf{f}$

1.2.1

The algebra ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ is the free $\mathbb{Q}(v)$-algebra with $1$ generated by \[ \{ \theta_i \mid i \in I \}. \]
For $\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$, set \[ {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}_\nu := \operatorname{Span}_{\mathbb{Q}(v)} \{ \theta_{i_1} \cdots \theta_{i_r} \mid i_1 + \cdots + i_r = \nu \}. \]Then, \[ {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f} = \bigoplus_{\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]} {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}_\nu \]is a $\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$-graded algebra.
A vector $x \in {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ is said to be homogeneous if $x \in {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}_\nu$ for some $\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$. In this case, set \[ |x| := \nu. \]

1.2.2

The tensor square ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}^{\otimes 2}$ admits the following $\mathbb{Q}(v)$-algebra structure: \[ (x_1 \otimes x_2) \cdot (y_1 \otimes y_2) := v^{|x_2| \cdot |y_1|} x_1y_1 \otimes x_2y_2. \]
The tensor cube ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}^{\otimes 3}$ admits the following $\mathbb{Q}(v)$-algebra structure: \[ (x_1 \otimes x_2 \otimes x_3) \cdot (y_1 \otimes y_2 \otimes y_3) := v^{|x_3| \cdot (|y_1| + |y_2|) + |x_2| \cdot |y_1|} x_1y_1 \otimes x_2y_2 \otimes x_3y_3. \]
There exists a unique algebra homomorphism \[ r : {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f} \rightarrow {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}^{\otimes 2} \]such that \[ r(\theta_i) = \theta_i \otimes 1 + 1 \otimes \theta_i. \]

Proposition 1.2.3

There exists a unique bilinear form $(,)$ on ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ satisfying the following.
  • $(1,1) = 1$.
  • $(\theta_i, \theta_j) = \delta_{i,j} \frac{1}{1-v_i^{-2}}$ for all $i,j \in I$.
  • $(x, y_1y_2) = (r(x), y_1 \otimes y_2)$ for all $x,y_1,y_2 \in {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$.
  • $(x_1x_2, y) = (x_1 \otimes x_2, r(y))$ for all $x_1,x_2,y \in {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$.
Here, \[ (x_1 \otimes x_2, y_1 \otimes y_2) := (x_1, y_1) \cdot (x_2, y_2). \]
Moreover, the form $(,)$ is symmetric.

1.2.4

The radical $\mathcal{I}$ of $(,)$ is a two-sided ideal of ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$.

1.2.5

Set \[ \mathbf{f} := {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}/\mathcal{I}. \]
It is $\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$-graded: \[ \mathbf{f} = \bigoplus_{\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]} \mathbf{f}_\nu, \quad \mathbf{f}_\nu := {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}_\nu + \mathcal{I}. \]
The form $(,)$ in Proposition 1.2.3 induces an inner product on $\mathbf{f}$.

1.2.7

There exists a unique anti-algebra automorphism $\sigma$ on ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ such that \[ \sigma(\theta_i) = \theta_i \text{ for all } i \in I. \]
We have \[ \sigma^2 = 1. \]

Lemma 1.2.8

We have \[ (\sigma(x), \sigma(y)) = (x,y) \text{ for all } x,y \in {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}. \]
This implies that $\sigma(\mathcal{I}) = \mathcal{I}$, and hence, the involution $\sigma$ induces a one on $\mathbf{f}$.

1.2.10

There exists a unique $\mathbb{Q}$-algebra automorphism $\overline{\cdot}$ on ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ such that \[ \overline{\theta_i} = \theta_i \text{ for all } i \in I, \quad \overline{v} = v^{-1}. \]
We have \[ \overline{\cdot}^2 = 1. \]

Lemma 1.2.11

We have \[ (\overline{x}, \overline{y}) = (-1)^{\operatorname{tr} |x|} v^{\frac{|x| \cdot |y|}{2}} v_{|x|} (x, \sigma(y)) \text{ for all } x,y \in {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}. \]
This implies that $\overline{\mathcal{I}} = \mathcal{I}$, and hence, the involution $\overline{\cdot}$ induces a one on $\mathbf{f}$.

1.2.13

Let $i \in I$. There exists a unique linear homomorphism ${}_i r : {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f} \rightarrow {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ such that \[ {}_ir(1) = 0,\ {}_ir(\theta_j) = \delta_{i,j},\ {}_ir(xy) = {}_ir(x)y + v^{i \cdot |x|} x \cdot {}_ir(y). \]
There exists a unique linear homomorphism $r_i : {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f} \rightarrow {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ such that \[ r_i(1) = 0,\ r_i(\theta_j) = \delta_{i,j},\ r_i(xy) = x r_i(y) + v^{|y| \cdot i} r_i(x)y. \]
We have \[ {}_ir(\mathcal{I}), r_i(\mathcal{I}) \subset \mathcal{I}. \]Hence, they induce linear endomorphisms on $\mathbf{f}$.

Lemma 1.2.15

Let $\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I] \setminus \{0\}$ and $x \in \mathbf{f}_\nu$.
  • If ${}_ir(x) = 0$ for all $i \in I$, then $x = 0$.
  • If $r_i(x) = 0$ for all $i \in I$, then $x = 0$.
