4.1. The Element $\Theta$

4.1.1

Let $\widehat{\mathbf{U} \otimes \mathbf{U}}$ denote the completion of the vector space $\mathbf{U} \otimes \mathbf{U}$ with respect to the descending sequence of vector spaces \[ \mathcal{H}_N := (\mathbf{U}^+ \mathbf{U}^0 \sum_{\operatorname{tr} \nu \geq N} \mathbf{U}^-_\nu \otimes \mathbf{U} + \mathbf{U} \otimes \mathbf{U}^- \mathbf{U}^0 \sum_{\operatorname{tr} \nu \geq N} \mathbf{U}^+_\nu) \]for $N \in \mathbb{Z}_{> 0}$.
The $\mathbb{Q}(v)$-algebra structure on $\mathbf{U} \otimes \mathbf{U}$ extends by continuity to a $\mathbb{Q}(v)$-algebra structure on $\widehat{\mathbf{U} \otimes \mathbf{U}}$.
Let $\overline{\cdot}$ denote the $\mathbb{Q}$-algebra involution $\overline{\cdot}^{\otimes 2}$ on $\mathbf{U} \otimes \mathbf{U}$. It extends by continuity to a $\mathbb{Q}$-algebra involution $\overline{\cdot}$.
Set \[ \overline{\Delta} := \overline{\cdot} \circ \Delta \circ \overline{\cdot}: \mathbf{U} \rightarrow \mathbf{U} \otimes \mathbf{U}. \]

Theorem 4.1.2

  • (a) There exists a unique family of elements $\Theta_\nu \in \mathbf{U}^-_\nu \otimes \mathbf{U}^+_\nu$, $\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$ such that
    • $\Theta_0 = 1 \otimes 1$.
    • $\Theta := \sum_{\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]} \Theta_\nu \in \widehat{\mathbf{U} \otimes \mathbf{U}}$ satisfies \[ \Delta(u) \Theta = \Theta \overline{\Delta}(u) \text{ in $\widehat{\mathbf{U} \otimes \mathbf{U}}$} \text{ for all } u \in \mathbf{U}. \]
  • (b) Let $B$ be a linear basis of $\mathbf{f}$ such that $B_\nu := \mathbf{f}_\nu \cap B$ is a basis of $\mathbf{f}_\nu$ for all $\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$. Let $\{ b^* \mid b \in B_\nu \}$ denote the dual basis of $B_\nu$. Then, we have \[ \Theta_\nu = (-1)^{\operatorname{tr} \nu} v_\nu \sum_{b \in B_\nu} b^- \otimes (b^*)^+. \]

Corollary 4.1.3

We have \[ \Theta \overline{\Theta} = \overline{\Theta} \Theta = 1 \otimes 1 \text{ in } \widehat{\mathbf{U} \otimes \mathbf{U}}. \]

4.1.4

The element $\Theta$ is called the quasi-$R$-matrix.
