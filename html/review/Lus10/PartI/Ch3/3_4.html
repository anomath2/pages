3.4. The Category $\mathcal{C}$

3.4.1

Define a category $\mathcal{C}$ as follows. An object of $\mathcal{C}$ is a $\mathbf{U}$-module $M$ with a given direct sum decomposition \[ M = \bigoplus_{\lambda \in X} M^\lambda \text{ (as a $\mathbb{Q}(v)$-vector space)} \]such that, for each $h \in Y$, $\lambda \in X$, and $m \in M^\lambda$, we have \[ K_h m = v^{\langle h, \lambda \rangle} m. \]The subspaces $M^\lambda$ are called the weight space of $M$.

3.4.4

For $M \in \mathcal{C}$, define a $\mathbf{U}$-module ${}^\omega M$ as follows. The $\mathbf{U}$-module ${}^\omega M$ has the same underlying vector space as $M$. The action of $u \in \mathbf{U}$ on ${}^\omega \mathbf{U}$ coincides with the action of $\omega(u)$ on $M$.

3.4.5

Let $\lambda \in X$. There exists a unique $\mathbf{U}$-module structure on $\mathbf{f}$ such that, for each homogeneous vector $y \in \mathbf{f}$, $h \in Y$, and $i \in I$, we have \[ K_h y = v^{\langle h, \lambda-\alpha(|y|) \rangle} y, \quad F_i y = \theta_i y, \quad E_i 1 = 0. \]This module is called a Verma module and is denoted by $M_\lambda$.
The Verma module $M_\lambda$ is an object of $\mathcal{C}$.

3.4.6

Let $M \in \mathcal{C}$, $m \in M^\lambda$ be such that $E_i m = 0$ for all $i \in I$. Then, there exists a unique $\mathbf{U}$-module homomorphism $M_\lambda \rightarrow M$ which sends $1$ to $m$.

3.4.7

Let $\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{hi}}$ denote the full subcategory of $\mathcal{C}$ whose objects are the $M$ with the following property: For each $m \in M$, there exists an integer $N \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ such that $x^+ m = 0$ for all $x \in \mathbf{f}_\nu$ with $\operatorname{tr} \nu \geq N$.
A quotient of $M_\lambda$ is an object of $\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{hi}}$.
