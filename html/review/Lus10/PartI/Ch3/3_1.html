3.1. The Algebras ${}^\prime \mathbf{U}$ and $\mathbf{U}$

3.1.1

The algebra $\mathbf{U}$ is the unital associative $\mathbb{Q}(v)$-algebra with generators \[ \{ E_i, F_i, K_h \mid i \in I,\ h \in Y \} \]subject to the following relations: For each $h,h' \in Y$, $i,j \in I$, $f(\theta_i \mid i \in I) \in \mathcal{I}$. \begin{align*} \begin{split} &K_0 = 1, \\ &K_h K_{h'} = K_{h + h'}, \\ &K_h E_i = v^{\langle h, \alpha_i \rangle} E_i K_h, \\ &K_h F_i = v^{\langle h, -\alpha_i \rangle} F_i K_h, \\ &[E_i, F_j] = \delta_{i,j} \frac{K_i - K_i^{-1}}{v_i - v_i^{-1}}, \\ &f(E_i \mid i \in I) = f(F_i \mid i \in I) = 0. \end{split} \end{align*}
There exists a unique algebra homomorphism \[ \cdot^\pm: \mathbf{f} \rightarrow \mathbf{U} \]such that \[ \theta_i^+ = E_i, \quad \theta_i^- = F_i \text{ for all } i \in I. \]Its image is denoted by $\mathbf{U}^\pm$.
For each $i \in I$ and $n \in \mathbb{Z}$, set \[ E_i^{(n)} := (\theta_i^{(n)})^+, \quad F_i^{(n)} := (\theta_i^{(n)})^- \]

3.1.2

A morphism of root data of the same type induces an algebra homomorphism.

3.1.3

There exists a unique algebra automorphism $\omega$ on $\mathbf{U}$ such that \[ \omega(E_i) = F_i, \quad \omega(F_i) = E_i, \quad \omega(K_h) = K_{-h} \text{ for all } i \in I,\ h \in Y. \]
There exists a unique anti-algebra automorphism $\sigma$ on $\mathbf{U}$ such that \[ \sigma(E_i) = E_i, \quad \sigma(F_i) = F_i, \quad \sigma(K_h) = K_{-h} \text{ for all } i \in I,\ h \in Y. \]

Lemma 3.1.4

There exists a unique algebra homomorphism \[ \Delta : \mathbf{U} \rightarrow \mathbf{U} \otimes \mathbf{U}, \]called the comultiplication, such that \begin{align*} \begin{split} &\Delta(E_i) = E_i \otimes 1 + K_i \otimes E_i, \\ &\Delta(F_i) = 1 \otimes F_i + F_i \otimes K_i^{-1}, \\ &\Delta(K_h) = K_h \otimes K_h \end{split} \end{align*}for all $i \in I$, $h \in Y$.

3.1.11

There exists a unique algebra homomorphism \[ \epsilon: \mathbf{U} \rightarrow \mathbb{Q}(v), \]called the counit, such that \[ \epsilon(E_i) = \epsilon(F_i) = 0, \quad \epsilon(K_h) = 1 \]for all $i \in I$, $h \in Y$.

3.1.12

There exists a unique $\mathbb{Q}$-algebra automorphism $\overline{\cdot}$ on $\mathbf{U}$ such that \[ \overline{E_i} = E_i, \quad \overline{F_i} = F_i, \quad \overline{K_h} = K_{-h}, \quad \overline{v} = v^{-1} \]for all $i \in I$, $h \in Y$.

3.1.13

Set \[ {}_\mathcal{A} \mathbf{U}^\pm := ({}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f})^\pm. \]
