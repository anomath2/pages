3.5. Integrable Objects of $\mathcal{C}$

3.5.1

An object $M \in \mathcal{C}$ is said to be integrable if for each $m \in M$ and $i \in I$, there exists $N \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ such that \[ E_i^N m = F_i^N m = 0. \]
Let $\mathcal{C}'$ denote the full subcategory of $\mathcal{C}$ whose objects are the integrable $\mathbf{U}$-modules.

3.5.2

  • (a) If $M,N \in \mathcal{C}'$, then $M \otimes N \in \mathcal{C}'$.
  • (b) If $M \in \mathcal{C}'$, then ${}^\omega M \in \mathcal{C}'$.

Proposition 3.5.4

Let $u \in \mathbf{U}$. If the vector $u$ acts on zero on every $M \in \mathcal{C}'$, then $u = 0$.

3.5.5

In the remainder of this page, the root datum is assumed to be $Y$-regular.
Set \[ X^+ := \{ \lambda \in X \mid \langle h_i, \lambda \rangle \geq 0 \text{ for all } i \in I \}. \]An elements of $X$ is said to be dominant if it belongs to $X^+$.

Proposition 3.5.6

Let $\lambda \in X^+$.
  • (a) The $\mathbf{U}^-$-submodule $\mathcal{T}_\lambda$ of the Verma module $M_\lambda$ generated by \[ \{ \theta_i^{\langle h_i, \lambda \rangle + 1} \mid i \in I \} \] is a $\mathbf{U}$-submodule of $M_\lambda$.
  • (b) $\Lambda_\lambda := M_\lambda/\mathcal{T}_\lambda \in \mathcal{C}'$.

3.5.7

The image of $1 \in M_\lambda$ in $\Lambda_\lambda$ is denoted by $\eta_\lambda$. The vector $\eta_\lambda$ considered as a vector of ${}^\omega \Lambda_\lambda$ is denoted by $\xi_{-\lambda}$.

3.5.8

Let $M \in \mathcal{C}'$, $m \in M^\lambda \setminus \{ 0 \}$ be such that $E_i m = 0$ for all $i \in I$. Then, \[ \lambda \in X^+. \]Moreover, there exists a unique $\mathbf{U}$-module homomorphism $\Lambda_\lambda \rightarrow M$ which sends $\eta_\lambda$ to $m$.
