3.3. Antipode

Lemma 3.3.1

  • (a) There exists a unique anti-algebra homomorphism $S$ on $\mathbf{U}$ such that \[ S(E_i) = -K_i^{-1} E_i, \quad S(F_i) = -F_i K_i, \quad S(K_h) = K_h \] for all $i \in I$, $h \in Y$.
  • (c) There exists a unique anti-algebra homomorphism $S'$ on $\mathbf{U}$ such that \[ S(E_i) = -E_i K_i^{-1}, \quad S(F_i) = -K_i F_i, \quad S(K_h) = K_h \] for all $i \in I$, $h \in Y$.
  • (e) $SS' = S'S = 1$.

3.3.2

The map $S$ (resp., $S'$) is called the antipode (resp., skew-antipode) of $\mathbf{U}$.

3.3.4

The quadruple $(\mathbf{U}, \Delta, \epsilon, S)$ is a Hopf algebra.
