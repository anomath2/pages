6.2. Complete Reducibility in $\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{hi}} \cap \mathcal{C}'$

Assumption

In this chapter, the root datum is $Y$- and $X$-regular.

Lemma 6.2.1

Let $M \in \mathcal{C}'$ be a nonzero quotient of the Verma module $M_\lambda$. Then,
  • (a) $\lambda \in X^+$.
  • (b) $M$ is simple.

Theorem 6.2.2

Let $M \in \mathcal{C}^\mathrm{hi} \cap \mathcal{C}'$. Then, it is a sum of simple $\mathbf{U}$-submodules.

Corollary 6.2.3

  • (a) For each $\lambda \in X^+$, the $\mathbf{U}$-module $\Lambda_\lambda$ is simple.
  • (b) If $\lambda, \lambda' \in X^+$, then the $\mathbf{U}$-modules $\Lambda_\lambda$ and $\Lambda_{\lambda'}$ are isomorphic if and only if $\lambda = \lambda'$.
  • (c) A $\mathbf{U}$-module in $\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{hi}} \cap \mathcal{C}'$ is a direct sum of simple modules of the form $\Lambda_\lambda$ for various $\lambda \in X^+$.
