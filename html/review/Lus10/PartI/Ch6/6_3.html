6.3. Affine or Finite Cartan Data

Assumption

In this chapter, the root datum is $Y$- and $X$-regular and either finite or affine type.

Proposition 6.3.2

Let $\lambda \in X$ be such that $\langle h_i, \lambda \rangle \leq -1$ for all $i \in I$. Then, the Verma module $M_\lambda$ is simple.

6.3.3

In the remainder of this section, we assume that $(I, \cdot)$ is of finite type.

Proposition 6.3.4

  • (a) For each $\lambda \in X^+$, we have \[ \dim \Lambda_\lambda < \infty. \]
  • (b) Let $M \in \mathcal{C}$ be such that $\dim M < \infty$. Then, $M \in \mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{hi}} \cap \mathcal{C}'$. Consequently (by 6.2.2), it is a direct sum of $\Lambda_\lambda$ for various $\lambda \in X^+$.

Proposition 6.3.6

Let $M \in \mathcal{C}'$. Then, it is aa sum of $\Lambda_\lambda$ for various $\lambda \in X^+$.
