Abstract

  • The algebra $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ is a modified form of $\mathbf{U}$ in which $\mathbf{U}^0$ is replaced by the direct sum of infinitely many one-dimensional algebras, one for ech element of $\mathcal{X}$.
  • The $\mathbf{U}$-modules in $\mathcal{C}$ can be regarded naturally as modules for $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$. Thus, $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ is an algebra more appropriate than $\mathbf{U}$ for the study of objects of $\mathcal{C}$.
  • $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ has a canonical basis $\dot{\mathbf{B}}$ with remarkable properties.
  • Chapter 24 defines a canonical basis of ${}\omega \Lambda_\lambda \otimes \Lambda_{\lambda'}$.
  • In Chapter 25, we show that these bases have some rather nice stability properties.
  • In Chapter 26, a characterization (up to signs) of $\dot{\mathbf{B}}$ is given.
  • In Chapter 29, we prove a refinement of the Peter-Weyl theorem.
