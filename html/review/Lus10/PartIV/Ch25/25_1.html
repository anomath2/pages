25.1. Stability Properties

Assumption

In this section, the root datum is $Y$-regular.

Proposition 25.1.2

Let $\lambda,\lambda' \in X^+$.
  • (a) There exists a unique $\mathbf{U}$-module homomorphism \[ \chi: \Lambda_{\lambda+\lambda'} \rightarrow \Lambda_\lambda \otimes \Lambda_{\lambda'} \] such that \[ \chi(\eta_{\lambda+\lambda'}) = \eta_\lambda \otimes \eta_{\lambda'}. \]
  • (b) Let $b \in \mathbf{B}(\lambda+\lambda')$. Then, we have \[ \chi(b^- \eta_{\lambda+\lambda'}) = \sum_{(b_1,b_2) \in \mathbf{B}(\lambda) \times \mathbf{B}(\lambda')} f(b;b_1,b_2) b_1^- \eta_\lambda \otimes b_2^- \eta_{\lambda'} \] for some $f(b;b_1,b_2) \in \mathbb{Z}[v^{-1}]$.
  • (c) If $b^- \eta_{\lambda'} \neq 0$, then \[ f(b;1,b) = 1 \text{ and } f(b;1,b_2) = 0 \text{ for all } b_2 \neq b. \] If $b^- \eta_{\lambda'} = 0$, then \[ f(b;1,b_2) = 0 \text{ for all } b_2. \]

Proposition 25.1.3

Let $\lambda,\lambda' \in X^+$.
  • (a) There exists a unique $\mathbf{U}$-module homomorphism \[ \chi': {}^\omega \Lambda_{\lambda+\lambda'} \rightarrow {}^\omega \Lambda_{\lambda'} \otimes {}^\omega \Lambda_{\lambda} \] such that \[ \chi'(\xi_{-\lambda-\lambda'}) = \xi_{-\lambda'} \otimes \xi_{-\lambda}. \]

Proposition 25.1.4

Let $\lambda \in X^+$.
  • (a) There exists a unique $\mathbf{U}$-module homomorphism \[ \delta_\lambda: {}^\omega \Lambda_\lambda \otimes \Lambda_\lambda \rightarrow \mathbb{Q}(v) \] such that \[ \delta_\lambda(\xi_{-\lambda} \otimes \eta_\lambda) = 1. \]
  • (b) Let $b,b' \in \mathbf{B}(\lambda)$. Then, $\delta_\lambda(b^+ \xi_{-\lambda} \otimes (b')^- \eta_\lambda)$ is equal to $1$ if $b = b' = 1$ and is in $v^{-1} \mathbb{Z}[v^{-1}]$ otherwise.

25.1.5

Let $\lambda,\lambda',\lambda'' \in X^+$. Set \[ t := (1 \otimes \delta_{\lambda'} \otimes 1) \circ (\chi' \otimes \chi): {}^\omega \Lambda_{\lambda+\lambda'} \otimes \Lambda_{\lambda'+\lambda''} \rightarrow {}^\omega \Lambda_{\lambda} \otimes \Lambda_{\lambda''}. \]

Proposition 25.1.10

  • (a) Let $b \in \mathbf{B}(\lambda)$, $b'' \in \mathbf{B}(\lambda'')$. Then, \[ t(b \diamondsuit b'')_{\lambda+\lambda', \lambda'+\lambda''} = (b \diamondsuit b'')_{\lambda,\lambda''}. \]
  • (b) Let $b \in \mathbf{B}(\lambda + \lambda')$, $b'' \in \mathbf{B}(\lambda'+\lambda'')$. Assume that either $b \notin \mathbf{B}(\lambda)$ or $b'' \notin \mathbf{B}(\lambda'')$. Then, \[ t(b \diamondsuit b'')_{\lambda+\lambda', \lambda'+\lambda''} = 0. \]
