25.2. Definition of the Basis $\dot{\mathbf{B}}$ of $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$

Theorem 25.2.1

Assume that the root datum is $Y$-regular. Let $\zeta \in X$, $b,b'' \in \mathbf{B}$.
  • (a) There exists a unique element $u := b \diamondsuit_\zeta b'' \in {}\mathcal{A} \dot{\mathbf{U}} 1_\zeta$ such that \[ u \cdot (\xi_{-\lambda} \otimes \eta_{\lambda''}) = (b \diamondsuit b'')_{\lambda,\lambda''} \] for all $\lambda,\lambda'' \in X^+$ with $b \in \mathbf{B}(\lambda)$, $b'' \in \mathbf{B}(\lambda'')$, and $\lambda''-\lambda = \zeta$.
  • (b) If $\lambda,\lambda'' \in X^+$ are such that $\lambda''-\lambda = \zeta$ and either $b \notin \mathbf{B}(\lambda)$ or $b'' \notin \mathbf{B}(\lambda'')$, then \[ u \cdot (\xi_{-\lambda} \otimes \eta_{\lambda''}) = 0. \]
  • (c) $\overline{u} = u$.
  • (d) $\{ b \diamondsuit_\zeta b'' \mid \zeta \in X, b,b'' \in \mathbf{B} \}$ forms a basis of ${}\mathcal{A} \dot{\mathbf{U}}$.

25.2.4

The basis of $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ above is called the canonical basis and is denoted by $\dot{\mathbf{B}}$.
