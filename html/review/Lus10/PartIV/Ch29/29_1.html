29.1. The Subsets $\dot{\mathbf{B}}[\lambda]$ of $\dot{\mathbf{B}}$

Assumption

In this chapter, the Cartan datum is of finite type.

29.1.1

Let $\beta \in \dot{\mathbf{B}}$. Then, there exists a unique $\zeta \in X$ such that \[ \beta \in \dot{\mathbf{U}} 1_\zeta. \]Choose $\lambda,\lambda'' \in X^+$ such that $\lambda''-\lambda = \zeta$ and $\langle h_i, \lambda \rangle$ is large enough for all $i \in I$. Then, there exists a unique $\lambda_1 \in X^+$ such that \[ \beta \cdot (\xi_{-\lambda} \otimes \eta_{\lambda''}) \in B[\lambda_1], \]where $B$ denotes the canonical basis of ${}^\omega \Lambda_\lambda \otimes \Lambda_{\lambda''}$. This $\lambda_1$ does not depend on the choice of $\lambda,\lambda''$.
Let $\dot{\mathbf{B}}[\lambda_1]$ denote the subset of $\dot{\mathbf{B}}$ consisting of elements which gives rise to $\lambda_1$ as above. Then, \[ \dot{\mathbf{B}} = \bigsqcup_{\lambda_1 \in X^+} \dot{\mathbf{B}}[\lambda_1]. \]

29.1.2

For each $\lambda_1 \in X^+$, let $\dot{\mathbf{U}}[\geq \lambda_1]$ (resp., $\dot{\mathbf{U}}[> \lambda_1]$) denote the subspace of $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ spanned by $\bigsqcup_{\lambda_2 \geq \lambda_1} \dot{\mathbf{B}}[\lambda_2]$ (resp., $\bigsqcup_{\lambda_2 > \lambda_1} \dot{\mathbf{B}}[\lambda_2]$).

Lemma 29.1.3

Let $u \in \dot{\mathbf{U}}$ and $\lambda_1 \in X^+$. Then, the following are equivalent.
  • (a) $u \in \dot{\mathbf{U}}[\geq \lambda_1]$.
  • (b) For each $\lambda,\lambda'' \in X^+$, we have $u \cdot (\xi_{-\lambda} \otimes \eta_{\lambda''}) \in ({}^\omega \Lambda_\lambda \otimes \Lambda_{\lambda''})[\geq \lambda_1]$.
  • (c) For each $M \in \mathcal{C}$ of finite dimension and $m \in M$, we have $um \in M[\geq \lambda_1]$.
  • (d) If $\lambda_2 \in X^+$ and $u$ acts on $\Lambda_{\lambda_2}$ by a nonzero linear map, then $\lambda_2 \geq \lambda_1$.

29.1.6

The $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$-module structure on $\Lambda_{\lambda_1}$ gives us a algebra homomorphism \[ \dot{\mathbf{U}} \rightarrow \operatorname{End}(\Lambda_{\lambda_1}). \]This restricts to \[ \dot{\mathbf{U}}[\geq \lambda_1] \rightarrow \operatorname{End}(\Lambda_{\lambda_1}), \]whose kernel is $\dot{\mathbf{U}}[> \lambda_1]$. Hence, we have an algebra homomorphism \[ \dot{\mathbf{U}}[\geq \lambda_1]/\dot{\mathbf{U}}[> \lambda_1] \rightarrow \operatorname{End}(\Lambda_{\lambda_1}), \]which is injective. In particular, \[ |\dot{\mathbf{B}}[\lambda_1]| = \dim \dot{\mathbf{U}}[\geq \lambda_1]/\dot{\mathbf{U}}[> \lambda_1] < \infty. \]
