29.5. The Quantum Coordinate Algebra

Assumption

In this chapter, the Cartan datum is of finite type.

29.5.1

Let $\mathbf{O}$ denote the vector space of all linear maps $f: \dot{\mathbf{U}} \rightarrow \mathbb{Q}(v)$ such that $f$ vanishes on $\dot{\mathbf{U}}[\geq \lambda]$ for some $\lambda \in X^+$.
Each $a \in \dot{\mathbf{B}}$ defines an element $\tilde{a} \in \mathbf{O}$ by \[ \tilde{a}(a') = \delta_{a,a'} \text{ for all } a' \in \dot{\mathbf{B}}. \]Then, $\{ \tilde{a} \mid a \in \dot{\mathbf{B}} \}$ forms a basis of $\mathbf{O}$, called the canonical basis.
