29.3. The Refined Peter-Weyl Theorem

Assumption

In this chapter, the Cartan datum is of finite type.

29.3.2

An element $\beta \in \dot{\mathbf{B}}$ is said to be involutive if $\sigma \omega(\beta) = \pm \beta$.

Theorem 29.3.3

Let $\lambda \in X^+$
  • (a) $\dot{\mathbf{U}}[\geq \lambda]$ and $\dot{\mathbf{U}}[> \lambda]$ are two-sided ideals of $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$, which are spanned by their intersections with $\dot{\mathbf{B}}$. The quotient algebra $\dot{\mathbf{U}}[\geq \lambda]/\dot{\mathbf{U}}[> \lambda]$ is isomorphic to $\operatorname{End} \Lambda_\lambda$. Let $\pi: \dot{\mathbf{U}}[\geq \lambda] \rightarrow \dot{\mathbf{U}}[\geq \lambda]/\dot{\mathbf{U}}[> \lambda]$ denote the quotient map.
  • (b) $\dot{\mathbf{U}}[\geq \lambda]/\dot{\mathbf{U}}[> \lambda]$ is semisimple as a left $\mathbf{U}$-module, and each irreducible summand is generated by its intersection with $\pi(\dot{\mathbf{B}}[\lambda])$.
  • (c) $\dot{\mathbf{U}}[\geq \lambda]/\dot{\mathbf{U}}[> \lambda]$ is semisimple as a right $\mathbf{U}$-module, and each irreducible summand is generated by its intersection with $\pi(\dot{\mathbf{B}}[\lambda])$.
  • (e) Each summand in the decomposition (b) and (c) contains a unique element of the form $\pi(\beta)$ for some involutive $\beta \in \dot{\mathbf{B}}[\lambda]$.
  • (f) Let $b,b' \in \dot{\mathbf{B}}[\lambda]$. Then, $bb' = c_{b,b',b''} b'' \pmod{\dot{\mathbf{U}}[> \lambda]}$ for some $b'' \in \dot{\mathbf{B}}[\lambda]$ and $c_{b,b',b''} \in \mathcal{A}$.
