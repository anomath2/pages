23.1. Definition and First Properties of $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$

23.1.1

For $\lambda', \lambda'' \in X$, set \[ {}_{\lambda'} \mathbf{U}_{\lambda''} := \mathbf{U}/(\sum_{h \in Y}(K_h - v^{\langle h, \lambda' \rangle})\mathbf{U} + \sum_{h \in Y} \mathbf{U}(K_h - v^{\langle h, \lambda'' \rangle})). \]Let \[ \pi_{\lambda', \lambda''} : \mathbf{U} \rightarrow {}_{\lambda'} \mathbf{U}_{\lambda''} \]denote the canonical projection.
Set \[ \dot{\mathbf{U}} := \bigoplus_{\lambda',\lambda'' \in X} {}_{\lambda'} \mathbf{U}_{\lambda''}. \]
Consider the direct sum decomposition \[ \mathbf{U} = \bigoplus_{\nu \in \mathbb{Z}[I]} \mathbf{U}(\nu) \]such that \[ \mathbf{U}(\nu') \mathbf{U}(\nu'') \subset \mathbf{U}(\nu'+\nu''),\ K_h \in \mathbf{U}(0),\ E_i \in \mathbf{U}(i),\ F_i \in \mathbf{U}(-i). \]
There exists a natural associative $\mathbb{Q}(v)$-algebra structure on $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ inherited from that of $\mathbf{U}$. \[ \pi_{\lambda_1',\lambda_1''}(t) \cdot \pi_{\lambda_2', \lambda_2''}(s) := \delta_{\lambda_1'', \lambda_2'} \pi_{\lambda_1', \lambda_2''}(ts) \]for all $\lambda_1',\lambda_1'',\lambda_2',\lambda_2'' \in X$, $t \in \mathbf{U}(\lambda_1'-\lambda_1'')$, $s \in \mathbf{U}(\lambda_2'-\lambda_2'')$.
The elements \[ 1_\lambda := \pi_{\lambda, \lambda}(1) \in \dot{\mathbf{U}} \]for $\lambda \in X$ satisfy \[ 1_\lambda 1_{\lambda'} = \delta_{\lambda, \lambda'} 1_\lambda. \]
We have \[ {}_{\lambda'} \mathbf{U}_{\lambda''} = 1_{\lambda'} \dot{\mathbf{U}} 1_{\lambda''}. \]

23.1.3

$\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ is naturally a $\mathbf{U}$-bimodule: \[ t' \cdot \pi_{\lambda',\lambda''}(s) \cdot t'' := \pi_{\lambda'+\alpha(\nu'), \lambda''-\alpha(\nu'')}(t' s t'') \]for all $\lambda',\lambda'' \in X$, $\nu',\nu'' \in \mathbb{Z}[I]$, $t' \in \mathbf{U}(\nu')$, $t'' \in \mathbf{U}(\nu'')$, and $s \in \mathbf{U}$.

23.1.4

A $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$-module $M$ is said to be unital if
  • (a) for each $m \in M$, we have $1_\lambda m = 0$ for all but finitely many $\lambda \in X$.
  • (b) for each $m \in M$, we have $\sum_{\lambda \in X} 1_\lambda m = m$.
If $M$ is a unital $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$-module, then it is an object of $\mathcal{C}$ by \[ M^\lambda := 1_\lambda M, \quad u \cdot m := (u 1_\lambda) m \text{ for all } \lambda \in X,\ m \in M^\lambda. \]In this way, to give a unital $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$-module is the same as to give an object of $\mathcal{C}$.

23.1.6

The algebra automorphism $\omega$ and the anti-algebra automorphism $\sigma$ on $\mathbf{U}$ induces a one on $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ such that \[ \omega(1_\lambda) = 1_{-\lambda}, \quad \sigma(1_\lambda) = 1_{-\lambda}. \]
The $\mathbb{Q}$-algebra automorphism $\overline{\cdot}$ on $\mathbf{U}$ induces a one on $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ such that \[ \overline{1_\lambda} = 1_\lambda. \]
