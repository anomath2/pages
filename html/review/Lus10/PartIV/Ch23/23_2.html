23.2. Triangular Decomposition, $\mathcal{A}$-form for $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$

23.2.1

The $\mathbf{U}$-bimodule structure on $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ induces a $\mathbf{f}$-bimodule structure on $\mathbf{U}$: \[ x \cdot u \cdot y := x^+ u y^- \text{ for all } x,y \in \mathbf{f},\ u \in \dot{\mathbf{U}}. \]

Lemma 23.2.2

  • (a) The $\mathcal{A}$-submodule of $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ spanned by \[ \{ x^+ 1_\lambda y^- \mid x,y \in {}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f} \} \] coincides with the $\mathcal{A}$-submodule of $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ spanned by \[ \{ x^- 1_\lambda y^+ \mid x,y \in {}_{\mathcal{A}}\mathbf{f} \}. \] We denote it by ${}_\mathcal{A} \dot{\mathbf{U}}$.
  • (b) The elements \[ \{ b^+ 1_\lambda (b')^- \mid b,b' \in \mathbf{B},\ \lambda \in X \} \] form a basis of ${}_\mathcal{A} \dot{\mathbf{U}}$. The elements \[ \{ b^- 1_\lambda (b')^+ \mid b,b' \in \mathbf{B},\ \lambda \in X \} \] form a basis of ${}_\mathcal{A} \dot{\mathbf{U}}$.
  • (c) ${}_\mathcal{A} \dot{\mathbf{U}}$ is an $\mathcal{A}$-subalgebra of $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ generated by \[ \{ E_i^{(n)} 1_\lambda, F_i^{(n)} 1_\lambda \mid i \in I,\ n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0},\ \lambda \in X \}. \]
