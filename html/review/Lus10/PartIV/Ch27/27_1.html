27.1. Isotypical Components

Assumption

The Cartan datum is of finite type.

27.1.1

Let $M \in \mathcal{C}$ be such that $\dim M < \infty$. Fore each $\lambda \in X^+$, let $M[\lambda]$ denote the sum of simple subobjects of $M$ that are isomorphic to $\Lambda_\lambda$, and set \[ M[\geq \lambda] := \bigoplus_{\lambda' \geq \lambda} M[\lambda'], \quad M[> \lambda] := \bigoplus_{\lambda' > \lambda} M[\lambda'] \]

27.1.2

A based module is an object $M \in \mathcal{C}$ of finite dimension with a given $\mathbb{Q}(v)$-basis $B$ such that
  • (a) $B \cap M^\zeta$ is a basis of $M^\zeta$ for all $\zeta \in X$.
  • (b) The $\mathcal{A}$-submodule ${}_\mathcal{A} M$ generated by $B$ is stable under ${}_\mathcal{A} \dot{\mathbf{U}}$.
  • (c) The $\mathbb{Q}$-linear involution $\overline{\cdot}$ on $M$ defined by \[ \overline{f b} = \overline{f} \cdot \overline{b} \text{ for all } f \in \mathbb{Q}(v),\ b \in B \] is compatible with the $\mathbf{U}$-module structure.
  • (d) The $\mathbf{A}$-submodule $L(M)$ generated by $B$, together with the image of $B$ in $L(M)/v^{-1} L(M)$ forms a basis at $\infty$ of $M$.

27.1.3

Let $\tilde{\mathcal{C}}$ denote the category whose objects are based modules, and whose morphisms are $\mathbf{U}$-module homomorphisms $f : M \rightarrow M'$ in $\mathcal{C}$ such that
  • (a) For each $b \in B$, we have $f(b) \in B' \cup \{0\}$.
  • (b) $B \cap \operatorname{Ker} f$ is a basis of $\operatorname{Ker} f$.

27.1.4

Let $(M, B)$ be a based module, and $M'$ be a $\mathbf{U}$-submodule of $M$ such that $M'$ is spanned by a subset $B' \subset B$. Then, $(M', B')$ is a based module. Moreover, $(M/M', \{b+M' \mid b \in B \setminus B' \})$ is a based module.

Proposition 27.1.8

Let $(M, B) \in \tilde{\mathcal{C}}$ and $\lambda \in X^+$. Then,
  • (a) $B \cap M[\geq \lambda]$ is a basis of $M[\geq \lambda]$.
  • (b) $B \cap M[> \lambda]$ is a basis of $M[> \lambda]$.
