Notes

  • The algebra $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ has appeared in [BLM90] in a geometric setting for type $A$, but its definition in the general case is the same as that in type $A$.
  • In [Kas93], Kashiwara conjectured the existence of a canonical basis of $\dot{\mathbf{U}}$ for finite type, and constructed a basis of the quantum coordinate algebra $\mathbf{O}$ in which the structure constants were in $\mathbb{Q}[v,v^{-1}]$.
  • The definition of $\dot{\mathbf{B}}$ in the general case was given in [Lus92].
  • The results in Chapters 29, 29, and 30 are new.
