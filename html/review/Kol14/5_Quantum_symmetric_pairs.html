5. Quantum symmetric pairs

Definition 5.1

For each $\mathbf{c} = (c_i)_{i \in I \setminus X} \in (\mathbb{K}(q)^\times)^{I \setminus X}$ and $\mathbf{s} = (s_i)_{i \in I \setminus X} \in \mathbb{K}(q)^{I \setminus X}$, define $B_{\mathbf{c},\mathbf{s}} = B_{\mathbf{c},\mathbf{s}}(X,\tau)$ to be the subalgebra of $U_q(\mathfrak{g}')$ generated by \[ \{ E_i,F_i \mid i \in X \} \sqcup \{ K_h \mid h \in Q^\Theta \} \sqcup \{ B_i \mid i \in I \setminus X \}, \]where \[ B_i := F_i + c_i \theta_q(F_i K_i) K_i^{-1} + s_i K_i^{-1}. \]

Proposition 5.2

Let $\mathbf{c} = (c_i)_{i \in I \setminus X} \in (\mathbb{K}(q)^\times)^{I \setminus X}$ and $\mathbf{s} = (s_i)_{i \in I \setminus X} \in \mathbb{K}(q)^{I \setminus X}$. Then, $B_{\mathbf{c},\mathbf{s}}$ is a right coideal subalgebra of $U_q(\mathfrak{g}')$.

Definition 5.6

We call $B_{\mathbf{c},\mathbf{s}}$ for $\mathbf{c} \in \mathcal{C}$, $\mathbf{s} \in \mathcal{S}$ a quantum symmetric pair coideal subalgebra of $U_q(\mathfrak{g}')$. If $\mathbf{s} = (0,\dots,0)$, then $B_{\mathbf{c},\mathbf{s}}$ is called standard. Otherwise, nonstandard.

5.4. References to Letzter's constructions

  • For finite dimensional $\mathfrak{g}$, standard and nonstandard quantum symmetric pairs were introduced in full generality in [Let02, Variants 1 and 2, after (7.25)].
