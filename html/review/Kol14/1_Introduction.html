1. Introduction

  • $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$, discovered by Drinfeld [Dr86] and Jimbo [Jim85], is an integral part of representation theory with many deep applications.
  • The present paper is concerned with the construction and the structure theory of quantum group analogues of $U(\mathfrak{k})$ as right coideal subalgebras $B = B(\theta)$ of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$. The pair $(U_q(\mathfrak{g}), B)$ is referred to as a quantum symmetric pair.
  • If $\mathfrak{g}$ is finite dimensional, then there exist two approaches to the construction of quantum symmetric pairs.
    • In the early nineties, Noumi, Sugitani, and Dijkhuizen constructed quantum group analogues of $U(\mathfrak{k})$ as coideal subalgebras of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ for all $g$ of classical type [NS95], [N96], [NDS97]. Their approach is based on explicit solutions of the reflection equation and hence follows in spirit the methods developed by the Leningrad school of mathematical physics [FRT88].
    • Letzter developed a comprehensive theory of quantum symmetric pairs for all semisimple Lie algebras [Let97], [Let99], [Let00], [Let02], [Let03], [Let04], [Let08]. Her theory is based on the Drinfeld-Jimbo presentation of quantized enveloping algebras.
    • The two approaches provide essentially the same coideal subalgebras of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ [Let99, Section 6].
  • Numerous examples of quantum symmetric pairs for infinite dimensional symmetrizable Kac-Moody algebras have appeared in the literature.

$q$-Onsager algebras

  • The $q$-Onsager algebra is a quantum symmetric pair coideal subalgebra for the Chevalley involution of the affine Lie algebra $\hat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2(\mathbb{K})$.
  • The Onsager algebra appeared in Onsager's investigation of the planar Ising model [O44].
  • The $q$-Onsager algebra appeared in Terwilliger's investigation of tridiagonal pairs and polynomial association schemes [T93].
  • The name $q$-Onsager algebra goes back to Baseilhac and Koizumi [BaKo05] who observed its role as a symmetry algebra for a class of quantum integrable models.
  • Study of generalized $q$-Onsager algebras was proposed in [BB10].

Twisted quantum loop algebras of the second kind

  • $\theta$ on $\mathfrak{g}$ lists to a one on $L(\mathfrak{g})$ by $\theta(x \otimes t^n) = \theta(x) \otimes t^{-n}$, and on $\hat{\mathfrak{g}}$. The fixed points subalgebra is called the twisted loop algebra.
  • Its quantum analogue is called the twisted quantum loop algebra of the second kind.
  • The most prominent examples of twisted quantum loop algebras were introduced by Molev, Regoucy, and Sorba [MRS03]; they call their algebras twisted $q$-Yangians.

Quantized GIM Lie algebras

  • Generalized intersection matrix (GIM) Lie algebras, originally introduced by Slodowy, form a class of Lie algebras which generalize Kac-Moody algebras by allowing Cartan matrices with positive off-diagonal entries.
  • Berman [B89] realized that GIM Lie algebras are subalgebras fixed under an involution of certain symmetrizable Kac-Moody algebras.
  • Quantum group analogues of GIM Lie algebras associated to two-fold affinizations of Cartan matrices of type ADE were constructed in an attempt to better understand the quantum toroidal algebras. The resulting quantized GIM Lie algebras are QSP coideal subalgebras.
