7. Generators and relations

Theorem 7.1

Let $\tilde{B}$ be the algebra freely generated over $\mathcal{M}_X^+ U_\Theta^{0 \prime}$ by elements $\tilde{B}_i$ for all $i \in I$ and $\Pi: \tilde{B} \rightarrow B_{\mathbf{c},\mathbf{s}}$ denote the canonical algebra homomorphism given by \[ \tilde{B}_i \mapsto B_i. \]Then, there exist elements \[ \tilde{C}_{i,j}(\mathbf{c}) \in \sum_{\operatorname{wt}(J) < \lambda_{i,j}} \mathcal{M}_X^+ U_\Theta^{0 \prime} \tilde{B}_J \text{ for all } i \neq j \in I \]such that $\operatorname{Ker} \Pi$ is the ideal of $\tilde{B}$ generated by the following elements:
  • $K_\beta \tilde{B}_i - q^{-(\beta,\alpha_i)} \tilde{B}_i K_\beta$ for all $\beta \in Q^\Theta$, $i \in I$.
  • $E_i \tilde{B}_j - \tilde{B_j} E_i - \delta_{i,j} \frac{K_i - K_i^{-1}}{q_i - q_i^{-1}}$ for all $i \in X$, $j \in I$.
  • $F_{i,j}(\tilde{B}_i, \tilde{B}_j) - \tilde{C}_{i,j}(\mathbf{c})$ for all $i \neq j \in I$.
Moreover, the formal expression of the elements $\tilde{C}_{i,j}(\mathbf{c})$ in $\sum_{\operatorname{wt}(J) < \lambda_{i,j}} \mathcal{M}_X^+ U_\Theta^{0 \prime} \tilde{B}_J$ is independent of the parameters $\mathbf{s}$.
Set \[ C_{i,j}(\mathbf{c}) := \Pi(\tilde{C}_{i,j}(\mathbf{c})). \]

Theorem 7.3

If $i \notin \{ j, \tau(i), \tau(j )\}$, then \[ C_{i,j}(\mathbf{c}) = 0. \]

Theorem 7.4

Let $i \neq j \in I \setminus X$.
  • If $a_{i,j} = 0$, then \[ C_{i,j}(\mathbf{c}) = \delta_{i,\tau(j)} \frac{c_i \mathcal{Z}_i - c_j \mathcal{Z}_j}{q_i - q_i^{-1}}. \]
  • If $a_{i,j} = -1$, then \[ C_{i,j}(\mathbf{c}) = \delta_{i,\tau(i)} q_i c_i \mathcal{Z}_i B_j - \delta_{i,\tau(j)}[2]_i (q_i c_j \mathcal{Z}_j + q_i^{-2} c_i \mathcal{Z}_i) B_i. \]
  • If $a_{i,j} = -2$, then \[ C_{i,j}(\mathbf{c}) = \delta_{i,\tau(i)} q_i[2]_i^2 c_i \mathcal{Z}_i(B_iB_j - B_jB_i) + \delta_{i,\tau(j)}[3]_i q_i(q_i^4-1)(q_i^{-8} c_i \mathcal{Z}_i - c_j \mathcal{Z}_j) B_i^2. \]

Remark 7.5

The method in the proof of Theorem 7.4 can be in principle used to determine the lower order terms for $a_{i,j} = -3,-4,\ldots$.

Theorem 7.8

Let $i \in I \setminus X$, $j \in X$.
  • If $a_{i,j} = 0$, then \[ C_{i,j}(\mathbf{c}) = 0. \]
  • If $a_{i,j} = -1$, then \[ C_{i,j}(\mathbf{c}) = \delta_{i,\tau(i)} c_i(\frac{q_i^2 B_j \mathcal{Z}_i - \mathcal{Z}_i B_j}{q_i-q_i^{-1}} + \frac{[2]_i \mathcal{W}_{i,j} K_j}{q_j-q_j^{-1}}). \]
  • If $a_{i,j} = -2$, then \begin{align*} \begin{split} C_{i,j}(\mathbf{c}) &= \delta_{i,\tau(i)} c_i \frac{q_i^2([3]_i B_iB_j - (q_i^2+2)B_jB_i)\mathcal{Z}_i - \mathcal{Z}_i((2+q_i^{-2})B_iB_j - [3]_i B_jB_i)}{q_i-q_i^{-1}} \\ &\quad - \delta_{i,\tau(i)} \frac{(q_i-q_i^{-1})[2]_i^2[3]_i B_i c_i \mathcal{W}_{i,j} K_j}{q_j-q_j^{-1}}. \end{split} \end{align*}
