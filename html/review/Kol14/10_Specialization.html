10. Specialization

Theorem 10.1

There exists an isomorphism of algebras \[ \mathcal{U}'_1 \rightarrow U(\mathfrak{g}') \]such that \[ \overline{E_i} \mapsto e_i, \quad \overline{F_i} \mapsto f_i, \quad \overline{(K_i;0)_q} \mapsto \epsilon_i h_i. \]

Proposition 10.2

$\theta_q(X,\tau)$ specializes to $\theta(X,\tau)$.

Corollary 10.3

If $c_i,s_i \in \mathbf{A}$ and $c_i(1) = 1$, then $B_i \in \mathcal{U}'_\mathbf{A}$ and \[ \overline{B_i} = f_i + \theta(f_i) + s_i(1). \]

Theorem 10.8

If $c_i,s_i \in \mathbf{A}$ and $c_i(1) = 1$ for all $i \in I \setminus X$, then \[ \overline{B_{\mathbf{c},\mathbf{s}}} = U(\mathfrak{k}'). \]

Theorem 10.11

Let $W$ be a subspace of $U_q(\mathfrak{g}')$ which contains $B_{\mathbf{c},\mathbf{s}}$ for some $\mathbf{c},\mathbf{s}$ such that $c_i,s_i \in \mathbf{A}$ and $c_i(1) = 1$ for all $i \in I \setminus X$. If $\overline{W} = U(\mathfrak{k}')$, then \[ W = B_{\mathbf{c},\mathbf{s}}. \]
