0. Introduction

The aim is to present a more or less coherent overview of a number of quantum analogues of symmetric spaces that have recently been studied. It was recognized not long after the introduction of quantum groups that the quantization of symmetric spaces was far from straightforward.

First of all, there was the, at first sight rather annoying, lack of interesting quantum subgroups. For instance, there is no quantum analogue of $SO(n)$ inside $SU_q(n)$. Koornwinder [K90] constructed a quantum analogue of the classical $2$-sphere $SU(2)/SO(2)$ and express the zonal spherical functions as a subclass of Askey-Wilson polynomials by an "infinitesimal" approach.
This "infinitesimal" approach was considerable generalized by Noumi in [N96], where he used the more general notion of a two-sided coideal in $\mathcal{U}_q(\mathfrak{g})$ to study quantum analogues of the symmetric spaces $SU(n)/SO(n)$ and $SU(2n)/Sp(n)$ and express their zonal spherical functions as Macdonald's symmetric polynomials associated with root system $A_{n-1}$.

A second rather striking phenomenon was the appearance of parametrized families of quantum symmetric spaces with one and the same classical counterpart. Podle\'{s} [P87] was the first to exhibit a continuously parametrized family of quantum analogues of the classical $2$-sphere. The zonal spherical functions on these quantum spheres were completely analyzed by Koornwinder [K90], [K93].
