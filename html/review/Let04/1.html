1. Introduction

A classical result shows that the zonal spherical functions associated to real compact symmetric spaces can be realized as Jacobi polynomials. There is an analogous result for $p$-adic symmetric spaces. The Macdonald polynomials provide a unified context for the polynomial families used in these parallel theories.
In the 1980s, Koornwinder and Macdonald asked whether one could obtain a similar description of quantum zonal spherical functions. This conjecture was investigated extensively in the 1990s by Noumi, Sugitani, Dijkhuizen, Stokman and others when the underlying Lie algebras is of classical type. The main result of this paper is an answer to Koornwinder and Macdonald's question for all quantum symmetric pairs with reduced restricted root systems, thus generalizing these earlier works to include Lie algebras of exceptional type.

The first problem faced in the quantum case was the actual definition of quantum symmetric pairs. The initial breakthrough was made by Koornwinder [K93], who constructed quantum $2$ spheres and computed their zonal spherical functions by inventing quantum analogues of the symmetric pair $(\mathfrak{sl}_2, \mathfrak{so}_2)$.
Noumi [N96] extended Koornwinder's approach to two families of symmetric pairs of type $A_n$, using two-sided coideal analogues of $\mathfrak{g}^\theta$ inside $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$. He showed the corresponding zonal spherical functions are Macdonald polynomials.
In [NS95], Noumi and Sugitani introduced one-sided coideal analogues of $U(\mathfrak{g}^\theta)$ inside of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ for other cases when $\mathfrak{g}$ is of classical type.
Dijkhuizen [D96] conjectures that coideals are the correct objects to use in order to develop a general theory of quantum symmetric pairs, the corresponding symmetric spaces, and their zonal spherical functions.
In [Let99] and [Let02], a universal method was developed for constructing left coideal subalgebras of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ which specialize to $U(\mathfrak{g}^\theta)$ as $q$ goes to $1$. A complete list of the generators and relations for all possible symmetric pairs is given in [Let03].

Let $\mathcal{B}_\theta$ denote the orbit of the analogues of $U(\mathfrak{g}^\theta)$ in $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ under the group $\mathbf{H}$ of Hopf algebra automorphisms of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ fixing the Cartan part. For each $B,B' \in \mathcal{B}_\theta$, one can associate the space ${}_{B'} \mathcal{H}_B$ of left $B'$ and right $B$ invariants inside the quantized function algebra $R_q[G]$ corresponding to $\mathfrak{g}$. Viewing elements of $R_q[G]$ as functions on $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$, restriction to the Cartan part of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ yields an algebra homomorphism $\Upsilon$ from ${}_{B'} \mathcal{H}_B$ into the character group ring associated to the restricted root system.
Zonal spherical functions are joint eigenvectors in ${}_{B'} \mathcal{H}_B$ with respect to the action of the center of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$.
