2.3. Convolutions

Given two $2\pi$-periodic integrable functions $f,g$, define their convolution $f*g$ by \[ (f * g)(x) := \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\pi}^\pi f(y) g(x-y) dy, \quad x \in [-\pi, \pi]. \]
Loosely speaking, convolutions correspond to "weighted averages". For instance, if $g = 1$, then $f * g$ is constant and equal to \[ \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_{-\pi}^\pi f(y) dy, \]which we may interpret as the average value of $f$ on the circle.

Proposition 3.1

Suppose that $f,g,h$ are $2\pi$-periodic integrable functions.
  • (i) $f * (g+h) = f*g + f*h$.
  • (ii) $(cf)*g = c(f*g) = f*(cg)$ for all $c \in \mathbb{C}$.
  • (iii) $f*g = g*f$.
  • (iv) $(f*g)*h = f*(g*h)$.
  • (v) $f*g$ is continuous.
  • (vi) $\widehat{f*g}(n) = \hat{f}(n) \hat{g}(n)$.
