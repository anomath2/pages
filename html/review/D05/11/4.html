11.4. A Reformulation

Let $K$ be a compact matrix group. The group $K \times K$ acts on the space $C(K)$ of continuous functions on $K$ by \[ (k_1, k_2) f(k) = f(k_1^{-1} k k_2). \]This action is unitary, and hence extends to $L^2(K)$.

Theorem 11.4.1

The matrix coefficients define a $K \times K$ isomorphism \[ L^2(K) \simeq \widehat{\bigoplus_{\tau \in \hat{K}_\mathrm{fin}}} \operatorname{End}(\tau), \]where $\hat{\oplus}$ means the Hilbert space completion of the algebraic direct sum.

Theorem 11.4.2

Every irreducible unitary representation of a compact matrix group $K$ is finite-dimensional, and thus $\hat{K}_\mathrm{fin}$ coincides with $\hat{K}$.
