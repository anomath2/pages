11.3. The Peter-Weyl Theorem

Let $K$ be a compact group, and $\hat{K}_\mathrm{fin}$ the set of isomorphism classes of finite-dimensional irreducible unitary representation of $K$.
For each $\tau \in \hat{K}_\mathrm{fin}$, choose an orthonormal basis $e_1,\dots,e_n$ of $\tau$, and set \[ \tau_{i,j}(k) := \langle k e_i, e_j \rangle \ \text{ for all } k \in K. \]The maps $\tau_{i,j} : K \rightarrow \mathbb{C}$ are called the matrix coefficients of $\tau$.

Theorem 11.3.1 (Peter-Weyl)

Let $\tau, \gamma \in \hat{K}_\mathrm{fin}$. Then, \[ \int_K \tau_{i,j}(k) \overline{\gamma_{r,s}(k)} dk = \delta_{\tau, \gamma} \delta_{i,r} \delta_{j,s} \frac{1}{\dim \tau}. \]The family \[ (\sqrt{\dim \tau} \tau_{i,j})_{\tau,i,j} \]forms an orthonormal basis of the Hilbert space $L^2(K)$.
