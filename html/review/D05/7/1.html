7.1. The Dual as LCA Group

Let $A$ be an LCA group. A character of $A$ is a continuous group homomorphism from $A$ to $\mathbb{T}$. The set of characters of $A$ is denoted by $\hat{A}$. It forms an abelian group, called the dual group or the Pontryagin dual of $A$.

Examples

  • $\hat{\mathbb{Z}} \simeq \mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}$.
  • $\widehat{\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}} \simeq \mathbb{Z}$.
  • $\hat{\mathbb{R}} \simeq \mathbb{R}$.

Let $A$ be an LCA group. Then, there is a natural metric on $\hat{A}$ such that $\hat{A}$ is an LCA group.
