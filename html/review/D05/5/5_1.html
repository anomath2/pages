5.1. The Dual Group

Let $A$ be a finite abelian group. A character $\chi$ of $A$ is a group homomorphism \[ \chi: A \rightarrow \mathbb{T} = \{ z \in \mathbb{C} \mid |z| = 1 \}. \]Let $\hat{A}$ be the set of characters of $A$.

Lemma 5.1.2

The pointwise product makes $\hat{A}$ an abelian group. We call $\hat{A}$ the dual group, or Pontryagin dual of $A$.

Theorem 5.1.4

Let $A$ be a finite abelian group. There is a canonical isomorphism \[ A \rightarrow \hat{\hat{A}};\ a \mapsto \delta_a, \]where \[ \delta_a(\chi) := \chi(a) \ \text{ for all } \chi \in \hat{A}. \]
