5.2. The Fourier Transform

Let $A$ be a finite abelian group. Then, the Hilbert space $\ell^2(A)$ coincides with $\mathbb{C}^A$. In particular, characters are elements of $\ell^2(A)$.

Lemma 5.2.1

Let $\chi,\eta \in \hat{A}$. Then, \[ \langle \chi, \eta \rangle = \delta_{\chi, \eta} |A|. \]

For $f \in \ell^2(A)$, define its Fourier transform $\hat{f} \in \ell^2(\hat{A})$ by \[ \hat{f}(\chi) := \frac{1}{\sqrt{|A|}} \langle f, \chi \rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{|A|}} \sum_{a \in A} f(a) \overline{\chi(a)}. \]

Theorem 5.2.2

Let $A$ be a finite abelian group. The map \[ \ell^2(A) \rightarrow \ell^2(\hat{A});\ f \mapsto \hat{f} \]is an isomorphism of Hilbert spaces.
