3.5. Plancherel's Theorem

Plancherel's theorem says that the Fourier transform preserves $L^2$-norms.

Let $L^2_\mathrm{bc}(\mathbb{R})$ denote the set of continuous, bounded functions $f: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{C}$ such that \[ ||f||_2^2 := \int_{-\infty}^\infty |f(x)|^2 dx < \infty. \]

Lemma 3.5.1

For $f,g \in L^2_\mathrm{bc}(\mathbb{R})$, the integral \[ \langle f, g \rangle := \int_{-\infty}^\infty f(x) \overline{g(x)} dx \]converges and defines an inner product on $L^2_\mathrm{bc}(\mathbb{R})$. The space $L^1_\mathrm{bc}(\mathbb{R})$ is a subspace of $L^2_\mathrm{bc}(\mathbb{R})$.

Let $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ denote the completion of the pre-Hilbert space $L^1_\mathrm{bc}(\mathbb{R})$ with respect to the inner product above.

Theorem 3.5.2 (Plancherel's theorem)

If $f \in L^1_\mathrm{bc}(\mathbb{R})$, then we have $\hat{f} \in L^2_\mathrm{bc}(\mathbb{R})$, and \[ ||f||_2 = ||\hat{f}||_2. \]In particular, the Fourier transform extends to a unitary map $L^2(\mathbb{R}) \rightarrow L^2(\mathbb{R})$.
