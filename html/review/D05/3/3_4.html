3.4. The Inversion Formula

The Fourier transform is, up to a sign twist, inverse to itself.

Theorem 3.4.4 (Inversion formula)

Let $f \in L^1_\mathrm{bc}(\mathbb{R})$ be such that $g := \hat{f} \in L^1_\mathrm{bc}(\mathbb{R})$. Then, for each $x \in \mathbb{R}$, we have \[ \hat{g}(x) = f(-x). \]
Another way to write this formula is \[ f(x) = \int_{-\infty}^\infty \hat{f}(y) e^{2\pi i xy} dy, \]which means that $f$ equals an integral over the plane waves $e^{2\pi i xy}$.

Corollary 3.4.5

The Fourier transform restricted to $\mathcal{S}$ gives a bijection of the set $\mathcal{S}$.
