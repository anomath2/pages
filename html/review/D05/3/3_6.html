3.6. The Poisson Summation Formula

We bring together Fourier analysis on $\mathbb{R}$ and on $\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}$ to derive the beautiful and expedient Poisson summation formula.

Theorem 3.6.1

Let $f \in L^1_\mathrm{bc}(\mathbb{R})$ be such that $f$ is a piecewise continuously differentiable with the possible exception of finitely many points. Let \[ \varphi(x) := \begin{cases} f'(x) & \text{ if it exists}, \\ 0 & \text{ otherwise}. \end{cases} \]Assume that $x^2 f(x)$ and $x^2 \varphi(x)$ are bounded. Then, the Poisson summation formula \[ \sum_{k \in \mathbb{Z}} f(k) = \sum_{k \in \mathbb{Z}} \hat{f}(k) \]holds.
