3.2. Convolution

Let \[ L^1_\mathrm{bc}(\mathbb{R}) \]denote the set of bounded continuous functions $f: \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{C}$ such that \[ ||f||_1 := \int_{-\infty}^\infty |f(x)| dx < \infty. \]

Theorem 3.2.1

Let $f,g \in L^1_{\mathrm{bc}}(\mathbb{R})$. Then, the integral \[ (f*g)(x) := \int_{-\infty}^\infty f(y) g(x-y) dy \]exists for every $x \in \mathbb{R}$. The function $f * g$ belongs to $L^1_\mathrm{bc}(\mathbb{R})$.

The function $f * g$ is called the convolution, or convolution product of $f,g$.
