[D05] 2.2. $\ell^2$-Spaces

Let $S$ be an arbitrary set. Let $\ell^2(S)$ be the set of functions $f: S \rightarrow \mathbb{C}$ satisfying \[ ||f||^2 := \sum_{s \in S} |f(s)|^2 < \infty. \]

Theorem 2.2.1

Let $S$ be a set. Then, the space $\ell^2(S)$ equipped with the inner product \[ \langle f, g \rangle := \sum_{s \in S} f(s) \overline{g(s)} \]is a Hilbert space.

Every Hilbert space is isomorphic to one of the form $\ell^2(S)$. And, two spaces $\ell^2(S)$ and $\ell^2(T)$ are isomorphic if and only if the sets $S$ and $T$ have the same cardinality.
