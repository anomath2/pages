[D05] 2.3. Orthonormal Bases and Completion

A complete system in a pre-Hilbert space $H$ is a family $(a_j)_{j \in J}$ of vectors in $H$ such that the linear subspace spanned by the vectors $a_j$ is dense in $H$.
A pre-Hilbert space is called separable if it contains a countable complete system.

Examples

For $j \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$, define $\psi_j \in \ell^2(\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0})$ by \[ \psi_j(k) = \delta_{j,k} \text{ for all } k \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}. \]Then, for each $f \in \ell^2(\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0})$, \[ \langle f, \psi_j \rangle = f(j). \]This implies that the family $(\psi_j)_{j \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}}$ is a complete system.

An orthonormal system in a pre-Hilbert space $H$ is a family $(h_j)_{j \in J}$ of vectors in $H$ such that \[ \langle h_j, h_k \rangle = \delta_{j,k} \text{ for all } j,k \in J. \]
An orthonormal system is called an orthonormal basis if it is also a complete system.

Example

The complete system $(\psi_j)_{j \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}}$ of $\ell^2(\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0})$ is an orthonormal basis.

Proposition 2.3.1

Every separable pre-Hilbert space admits an orthonormal basis. (The assertion also holds for nonseparable Hilbert spaces.)

Theorem 2.3.3

For every separable pre-Hilbert space $V$, there exists a Hilbert space $H$ such that there is an isometry \[ T: V \rightarrow H, \]called completion, that maps $V$ onto a dense subspace of $H$. The completion is unique up to isomorphism. (The assertion also holds for nonseparable Hilbert spaces.)
