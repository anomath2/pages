[D05] 2.1. Pre-Hilbert and Hilbert Spaces

A complex vector space $V$ together with an inner product $\langle , \rangle$ is called a pre-Hilbert space.

Examples

For each $k \in \mathbb{Z}_{> 0}$, the vector space $\mathbb{C}^k$ with natural inner product \[ \langle v, w \rangle = v \cdot {}^t \overline{w} \]is a pre-Hilbert space. Every $k$-dimensional pre-Hilbert space is isomorphic to $\mathbb{C}^k$.

A linear map $T: V \rightarrow W$ of two pre-Hilbert spaces $V,W$ is called an isometry if $T$ preserves inner products: \[ \langle T(u), T(v) \rangle = \langle u, v \rangle \text{ for all } u,v \in V. \]
Isometries are injective.
If an isometry is surjective, then it has an inverse, which is also an isometry. In this case, the isometry is called a unitary map or an isomorphism of pre-Hilbert spaces.

Let $V$ be a pre-Hilbert space and $v \in V$. We say that a sequence $(v_n)$ in $V$ converges to $v$ if \[ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} ||v_n - v|| = 0. \]In this case, we write \[ v = \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} v_n. \]

A subset $D \subset V$ is called a dense subset if for each $v \in V$, there exists a sequence $(d_n)$ in $D$ such that \[ v = \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} d_n. \]

A Cauchy sequence in $V$ is a sequence $(v_n)$ in $V$ such that for each $\epsilon > 0$, there exists $n_0 \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ such that for each $n,m \geq n_0$, we have \[ ||v_n - v_m|| < \epsilon. \]

A Hilbert space is a pre-Hilbert space which is complete, i.e., every Cauchy sequence converges.

Proposition 2.1.4

A finite-dimensional pre-Hilbert space is a Hilbert space.
