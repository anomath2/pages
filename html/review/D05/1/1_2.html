[D05] 1.2. Exponentials

For $k \in \mathbb{Z}$, set \[ e_k(x) = e^{2 \pi i k x}. \]Then, \[ e_k \in C(\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}). \]

Lemma 1.2.1

If $k,l \in \mathbb{Z}$, then \[ \langle e_k, e_l \rangle = \delta_{k,l}. \]

Let $f$ be periodic and Riemann integrable on the interval $[0,1]$. The numbers \[ c_k(f) := \langle f, e_k \rangle = \int_0^1 f(x) e^{-2 \pi i k x} dx, \ k \in \mathbb{Z}, \]are called the Fourier coefficients of $f$.
The (formal) series \[ \sum_{k = -\infty}^\infty c_k(f) e_k(x) \]is called the Fourier series of $f$.

Let $R(\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z})$ denote the subspace of periodic functions that are Riemann integrable on $[0,1]$. Then, \[ C(\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}) \subset R(\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}). \]

For $f \in C(\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z})$, set \[ ||f||_2 := \sqrt{\langle f, f \rangle}. \]
