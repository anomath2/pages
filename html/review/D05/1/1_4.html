[D05] 1.4. Convergence in the $L^2$-Norm

The notion of $L^2$-convergence is the appropriate notion of convergence for Fourier series.
Let $f, f_n \in R(\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z})$, $n \in \mathbb{Z}$. We say that the sequence $(f_n)$ converges in the $L^2$-norm to $f$ if \[ \lim_{n \rightarrow \infty} ||f - f_n||_2 = 0. \]Note that if $(f_n)$ converges to $f$ in the $L^2$-norm, then it need not converge pointwise, and vice versa.

Proposition 1.4.1

If the sequence $(f_n)$ converges to $f$ uniformly on $[0,1]$, then it converges to $f$ in the $L^2$-norm.

Theorem 1.4.4

Let $f \in R(\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z})$. Then, the Fourier series of $f$ converges to $f$ in the $L^2$-norm. If $c_k$ denotes the Fourier coefficients of $f$, then \[ \sum_{k=-\infty}^\infty |c_k|^2 = \int_0^1 |f(x)|^2 dx. \]
