6.2. Poincar\'{e}-Birkhoff-Witt Theorem and Verma Modules

6.2.1. Braid Groups

If $A = (a_{i,j})$ be the Cartan matrix of $\mathfrak{g}$, then the numbers $a_{ij} a_{ji}$ may be equal to $0,1,2,3$. Let $m_{ij}$ be equal to $2,3,4,6$ when $a_{ij}a_{ji}$ is equal to $0,1,2,3$, respectively.
The Weyl group $W$ of $\mathfrak{g}$ is defined as the group generated by the reflections $w_1,\dots,w_l$ satisfying the defining relations \[ w_i^2 = 1, \quad w_i w_j w_i \cdots = w_j w_i w_j \cdots \text{ if } i \neq j, \]where in the latter equations there are $m_{i,j}$ factors on each side.

Definition 3

The braid group of $\mathfrak{g}$ is the group $\mathfrak{B}_\mathfrak{g}$ generated by $s_1,\dots,s_l$ subject to the following relations: \[ s_is_js_i \cdots = s_js_is_j \cdots \text{ if } i \neq j, \]where there are $m_{i,j}$ factors on each side.

6.2.2. Action of Braid Groups on Drinfeld-Jimbo Algebras

Theorem 22

To every $i = 1,\dots,l$, there corresponds an algebra automorphism $\mathcal{T}_i$ on $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ which acts on the generators as \begin{align*} \begin{split} &\mathcal{T}_i(K_j) = K_jK_i^{-a_{i,j}}, \\ &\mathcal{T}_i(E_i) = -F_i K_i, \\ &\mathcal{T}_i(F_i) = -K_i^{-1} E_i, \\ &\mathcal{T}_i(E_j) = \sum_{r=0}^{-a_{i,j}} (-1)^{r-a_{i,j}} q_i^{-r} E_i^{(-a_{i,j}-r)} E_j E_i^{(r)}, \text{ if } i \neq j, \\ &\mathcal{T}_i(F_j) = \sum_{r=0}^{-a_{i,j}} (-1)^{r-a_{i,j}} q_i^{r} F_i^{(r)} F_j F_i^{(-a_{i,j}-r)}, \text{ if } i \neq j, \\ \end{split} \end{align*}where \[ E_i^{(n)} := \frac{E_i^n}{[n]_{q_1}!}. \]The mapping $s_i \mapsto \mathcal{T}_i$ determines a homomorphism of the braid group $\mathfrak{B}_\mathfrak{g}$ into the group of algebra automorphisms of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$.

6.2.3. Root Vectors and Poincar\'{e}-Birkhoff-Witt Theorem

Let $w_0 \in W$ be the longest element. Let $w_0 = w_{i_1} \cdots w_{i_n}$ be a fixed reduced decomposition. The sequence \[ \beta_1 = \alpha_{i_1}, \beta_2 = w_{i_1}(\alpha_{i_2}), \dots, \beta_n = w_{i_1} \cdots w_{i_{n-1}}(\alpha_{i_n}) \]exhausts all positive roots of $\mathfrak{g}$.

Definition 4

The elements \[ E_{\beta_r} := \mathcal{T}_{i_1} \cdots \mathcal{T}_{i_{r-1}}(E_{i_r}), \quad F_{\beta_r} := \mathcal{T}_{i_1} \cdots \mathcal{T}_{i_{r-1}}(F_{i_r}) \]are called root vectors of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ corresponding to the roots $\beta_r$ and $-\beta_r$, respectively.

The root vectors of $U(\mathfrak{g})$ can be defined in the same way using the Weyl group instead of the braid group. Then, different reduced decomposition of $w_0$ give, up to signs, the same root vectors of $U(\mathfrak{g})$. This is no longer true for the root vectors of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$.

Proposition 23

  • (i) If $w \in W$ and $\alpha_i$ are such that $w(\alpha_i) \in \Delta_+$, then $\mathcal{T}_w(E_i) \in U_q(\mathfrak{n}_+)$ and $\mathcal{T}_w(F_i) \in U_q(\mathfrak{n}_-)$, respectively.
  • (ii) If $w(\alpha_i) = \alpha_j$, then $\mathcal{T}_w(E_i) = E_j$ and $\mathcal{T}_w(F_i) = F_j$.

Theorem 24

The following set of elements is a basis of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$: \[ F_{\beta_1}^{r_1} \cdots F_{\beta_n}^{r_n} K_1^{t_1} \cdots K_l^{t_l} E_{\beta_n}^{s_n} \cdots E_{\beta_1}^{s_1}, \quad r_j, s_j \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}, t_j \in \mathbb{Z}. \]
