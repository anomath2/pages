6.1 Definitions of Drinfeld-Jimbo Algebras

6.1.2. The Drinfeld-Jimbo Algebras $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$

Let $\mathfrak{g}$ be a finite-dimensional complex semisimple Lie algebra of rank $l$ with Cartan matrix $(a_{i,j})_{1 \leq i,j \leq l}$ and $d_i := \frac{(\alpha_i, \alpha_i)}{2}$. Let $q$ be a fixed nonzero number and set \[ q_i := q^{d_i}. \]Suppose that $q_i^2 \neq 1$ for all $i = 1,\dots,l$.
Let $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ be the associative unital complex algebra with generators $E_i$, $F_i$, $K_i^{\pm 1}$, $1 \leq i \leq l$, and defining relations \begin{align*} \begin{split} &K_i K_i^{-1} = K_i^{-1} K_i = 1, \\ &K_i K_j = K_j K_i, \\ &K_i E_j K_i^{-1} = q_i^{a_{i,j}} E_j, \\ &K_i F_j K_i^{-1} = q_i^{-a_{i,j}} F_j, \\ &E_i F_j - F_j E_i = \delta_{i,j} \frac{K_i - K_i^{-1}}{q_i - q_i^{-1}}, \\ &\sum_{r=0}^{1-a_{i,j}} (-1)^r \left\lbrack\!{1-a_{i,j} \brack r}\!\right\rbrack_{q_i} E_i^{1-a_{i,j}-r} E_j E_i^r = 0 \text{ if } i \neq j, \\ &\sum_{r=0}^{1-a_{i,j}} (-1)^r \left\lbrack\!{1-a_{i,j} \brack r}\!\right\rbrack_{q_i} F_i^{1-a_{i,j}-r} F_j F_i^r = 0 \text{ if } i \neq j, \end{split} \end{align*}where \[ \left\lbrack\!{n \brack r}\!\right\rbrack_q := \frac{[n]_q!}{[r]_q! [n-r]_q!}, \quad [n]_q := \frac{q^n-q^{-n}}{q-q^{-1}}. \]

Proposition 5

There is a unique Hopf algebra structure on $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ such that \begin{align*} \begin{split} &\Delta(K_i) = K_i \otimes K_i, \\ &\Delta(E_i) = E_i \otimes K_i + 1 \otimes E_i, \\ &\Delta(F_i) = F_i \otimes 1 + K_i^{-1} \otimes F_i, \\ &\epsilon(K_i) = 1, \quad \epsilon(E_i) = \epsilon(F_i) = 0, \\ &S(K_i) = K_i^{-1}, \quad S(E_i) = -E_i K_i^{-1}, \quad S(F_i) = -K_i F_i. \end{split} \end{align*}

Definition 1

The Hopf algebra $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ is called the Drinfeld-Jimbo quantized universal enveloping algebra, or the Drinfeld-Jimbo algebra corresponding to the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ and the complex number $q$.

6.1.4. Some Algebra Automorphisms of Drinfeld-Jimbo Algebras

Proposition 9

Let $\epsilon = (\epsilon_1,\dots,\epsilon_l)$, $\epsilon_i = \pm 1$, and $\alpha = (\alpha_1,\dots,\alpha_l) \in \mathbb{C}^l$, $\alpha_i \neq 0$. There are algebra automorphisms $\theta$ and $\vartheta_{\epsilon \alpha}$ of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ such that \begin{align*} \begin{split} &\theta(E_i) = F_i, \quad \theta(F_i) = E_i, \quad \theta(K_i) = K_i^{-1}, \\ &\vartheta_{\epsilon \alpha}(E_i) = \alpha_i E_i, \quad \vartheta(F_i) = \epsilon_i \alpha_i^{-1} F_i, \quad \vartheta_{\epsilon \alpha}(K_i) = \epsilon_i K_i. \end{split} \end{align*}

6.1.5. Triangular Decomposition of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$

Let $U_q(\mathfrak{n}_+)$, $U_q(\mathfrak{n}_-)$, and $U_q(\mathfrak{h})$ be the subalgebra of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ generated by $E_i$, $F_i$, and $K_i^{\pm 1}$, respectively.
It is clear that $U_q(\mathfrak{n}_+)$ has a basis consisting of monomials $E_{i_1}^{n_1} \cdots E_{i_k}^{n_k}$. Let us fix such a basis and denote it by $\{ \mathbf{E}_r \mid r \in I \}$, where $I$ is an appropriate set of indices. Set \[ \mathbf{F}_r := \theta(\mathbf{E}_r). \]

Theorem 14

The elements $\mathbf{E}_r K_\gamma \mathbf{F}_{r'}$, $r,r' \in I$, $\gamma \in Q$ form a basis of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$. The mapping \[ U_q(\mathfrak{n}_+) \otimes U_q(\mathfrak{h}) \otimes U_q(\mathfrak{n}_-) \rightarrow U_q(\mathfrak{g}); \mathbf{E} \otimes K_\gamma \otimes \mathbf{F}_{r'} \mapsto \mathbf{E}_r K_\gamma \mathbf{F}_{r'} \]gives a linear isomorphism.

6.1.6. Hopf Algebra Automorphisms of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$

In this subsection, we suppose that $q$ is not a root of unity.
A permutation $\mu$ of the set $\{ 1,\dots, l \}$ such that \[ a_{\mu(i), \mu(j)} = a_{i,j} \]is called a diagram automorphism of $\mathfrak{g}$.

Theorem 16

Let $c_1,\dots,c_l \in \mathbb{C}^\times$ and $\mu$ be a diagram automorphism of $\mathfrak{g}$. Then, there is a unique Hopf algebra automorphism $\psi$ of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ such that \[ \psi(K_i) = K_{\mu(i)}, \quad \psi(E_i) = c_i E_{\mu(i)}, \quad \psi(F_i) = c_i^{-1} F_{\mu(i)}. \]Every Hopf algebra automorphism of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ is of this form.
