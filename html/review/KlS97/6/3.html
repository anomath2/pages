6.3. The Quantum Killing Form and the Center of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$

Except for Proposition 34 and Lemma 35 we assume that $q$ is not a root of unity.

6.3.1. A Dual Pairing of the Hopf Algebras $U_q(\mathfrak{b}_+)$ and $U_q(\mathfrak{b}_-)^{\mathrm{op}}$

Proposition 34

There is a unique dual pairing \[ \langle , \rangle: U_q(\mathfrak{b}_+) \times U_q(\mathfrak{b}_-)^{\mathrm{op}} \rightarrow \mathbb{C} \]of the Hopf algebras such that \[ \langle K_i, K_j \rangle = q^{-(\alpha_i, \alpha_j)}, \quad \langle K_j, F_i \rangle = \langle E_j, K_i \rangle = 0, \quad \langle E_j, F_i \rangle = \frac{\delta_{i,j}}{q_i^{-1}-q_i}. \]

6.3.2. The Quantum Killing Form on $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$

There is a well-defined bilinear form $\langle , \rangle{}_U$ on $U_q(\mathfrak{g}) \times U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ given by \[ \langle a_1 K_\alpha b_1, a_2 K_\beta b_2 \rangle{}_U := q^{-(\alpha,\beta)/2} \langle S(b_1), a_2 \rangle \langle b_2, S(a_1) \rangle \]for $a_1, a_2 \in U_q(\mathfrak{n}_-)$, $b_1,b_2 \in U_q(\mathfrak{n}_+)$, and $\alpha,\beta \in Q$.

Theorem 39

The bilinear form $\langle , \rangle{}_U$ on $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ is $\operatorname{ad}_L$-invariant, that is, \[ \langle \operatorname{ad}_L(a)b_1, b_1 \rangle{}_U = \langle b_1, \operatorname{ad}_L(S(a))b_2 \rangle{}_U \text{ for all } a,b_1,b_2 \in U_q(\mathfrak{g}). \]

Definition 9

The bilinear form $\langle , \rangle{}_U$ is called the quantum Killing form or the Rosso form of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$.
