[NS95] Quantum symmetric spaces and related $q$-orthogonal polynomials

Abstract

A class of quantum analogues of compact symmetric spaces of classical type is introduced by means of constant solutions to the reflection equations. Their zonal spherical functions are discussed in connection with $q$-orthogonal polynomials

Main motives:
  • How can one define the analogue of homogeneous spaces $G/K$ in the framework of quantum groups?
  • What do their spherical functions look like?
We will introduce a class of quantum analogues of compact symmetric spaces $G/K$ of classical type and study their zonal spherical functions. They are expected to provide a good class of $q$-orthogonal polynomials in many variables.
We have not yet reached an abstract definition of quantum symmetric spaces. We provide instead a practical method to construct examples of quantum symmetric spaces of classical type, by means of constant solutions to the reflection equations. In many examples, the zonal spherical functions are expressed by the Macdonald polynomials or Koornwinder's Askey-Wilson polynomials for $BC_l$.
