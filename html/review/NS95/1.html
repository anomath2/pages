1. Recalls on compact quantum groups

Let $G$ be one of the compact classical groups $SU(N)$, $SO(N)$, $Sp(N)$, and $\mathfrak{g}$ the Lie algebra of $G$.
  • $q \in \mathbb{R}$ with $0 < q < 1$.
  • $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$: $q$-deformation of $U(\mathfrak{g})$.
  • $A_q(G)$: $q$-deformation of the algebra of regular functions $A(G)$ on $G$.
The algebra $A_q(G)$ is the subalgebra of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})^* = \operatorname{Hom}_\mathbb{C}(U_q(\mathfrak{g}), \mathbb{C})$ generated by the matrix elements of the vector representation. Hence, we have the natural pairing of Hopf $*$-algebras \[ (,): U_q(\mathfrak{g}) \times A_q(G) \rightarrow \mathbb{C}. \]Then, the left and right $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$-module structure on $A_q(G)$ given by \[ a \varphi = (a, \Delta(\varphi))_2,\ \varphi a = (a, \Delta(\varphi))_1 \ \text{ for all } (a, \varphi) \in U_q(\mathfrak{g}) \times A_q(G), \]correspond to the left and right regular representations of $G$ on $A(G)$, respectively.
The $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$-bimodule $A_q(G)$ has the following irreducible decomposition of Peter-Weyl type: \[ A_q(G) = \bigoplus_{\lambda \in P_G^+} W(\lambda), \ W(\lambda) \simeq V(\lambda)^\vee \otimes V(\lambda). \]
The subspace $W(\lambda)$ is characterized by the action of the center $\mathcal{Z} U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$. For each $\lambda \in P_G^+$, let \[ \chi_\lambda : \mathcal{Z} U_q(\mathfrak{g}) \rightarrow \mathbb{C} \]denote the central character. Then, \[ W(\lambda) = \{ \varphi \in A_q(G) \mid C \varphi = \chi_\lambda(C) \varphi \text{ for all } C \in \mathcal{Z} U_q(\mathfrak{g}) \}. \]

Let \[ h: A_q(G) \rightarrow W(0) = \mathbb{C} \]denote the projection. We call it the normalized Haar function of the quantum group $G_q$.
Define a scalar product on $A_q(G)$ by \[ \langle \varphi | \psi \rangle = h(\varphi^* \psi) \ \text{ for all } \varphi, \psi \in A_q(G). \]The Peter-Weyl decomposition is orthogonal under this scalar product.
