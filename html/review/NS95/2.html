2. Quantum analogue of $G/K$ and $K \setminus G/K$

Let $K \subset G$ a closed subgroup, and $\mathfrak{k}$ its Lie algebra.
What is the appropriate definition of a quantum subgroup? What should be the quantum analogue of the pair $(U(\mathfrak{g}), U(\mathfrak{k}))$?

There does not seem to exist a Hopf subalgebra of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ which tends $U(\mathfrak{k})$ as $q \rightarrow 1$.

We propose to consider coideal subalgebras $U_q^{\mathrm{tw}}(\mathfrak{k})$ which tends $U(\mathfrak{k})$ as $q \rightarrow 1$. Set \[ \mathcal{J} := \sum_{a \in U_q^{\mathrm{tw}}(\mathfrak{k})} U_q(\mathfrak{g})(a - \epsilon(a)). \]Then, \[ A_q(G/K) := \{ \varphi \in A_q(G) \mid \mathcal{J} \varphi = 0 \} \]is a quantum analogue of the algebra of right $K$-invariant regular functions on $G$. The coideal property of $U_q^{\mathrm{tw}}(\mathfrak{k})$ guarantees that the subspace $A_q(G/K)$ is a subalgebra of $A_q(G)$.

We will mainly consider the case when the pair $(U_q(\mathfrak{g}), U_q^{\mathrm{tw}}(\mathfrak{k}))$ is a Gelfand pair in the sense that \[ \dim V(\lambda)^{\mathcal{J}} \leq 1 \ \text{ for all } \lambda \in P_G^+, \]where \[ V(\lambda)^\mathcal{J} := \{ v \in V(\lambda) \mid \mathcal{J} v = 0 \}. \]
Then, we obtain the following multiplicity-free decomposition \[ A_q(G/K) \simeq \bigoplus_{\lambda \in P_{G, \mathcal{J}}^+} V(\lambda)^\vee, \]where $P_{G, \mathcal{J}}^+$ is the subset of $P_G^+$ consisting of all $\lambda$ for which $V(\lambda)$ has nonzero $\mathcal{J}$-fixed vectors.

By using the group-like element $q^\rho \in U_q(\mathfrak{g})$, corresponding to the Weyl vector $\rho$, we modify the $*$-operation of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ as \[ \overline{a} := q^\rho a^* q^{-\rho}. \]Set \[ A_q(K \setminus G/K) := \{ \varphi \in A_q(G) \mid \mathcal{J} \varphi = \varphi \overline{\mathcal{J}} = 0 \}. \]Then, this subspace is a subalgebra of $A_q(G)$, and can be seen as a quantum analogue of the algebra of $K$-biinvariant regular functions on $G$.
We have a decomposition \[ A_q(K \setminus G/K) = \bigoplus_{\lambda \in P_{G, \mathcal{J}}^+} \mathcal{H}(\lambda), \ \mathcal{H}(\lambda) := W(\lambda) \cap A_q(K \setminus G/K) \]into one-dimensional subspaces. This also gives the eigenspace decomposition of the action of $\mathcal{Z} U_q(\mathfrak{g})$. We call a nonzero element $\varphi_\lambda \in \mathcal{H}(\lambda)$ a zonal spherical function on the quantum homogeneous space $(G/K)_q$ associated with the representation $V(\lambda)$. The element $\varphi_\lambda$ is characterized (up to a constant multiple) by the conditions \[ \mathcal{J} \varphi_\lambda = \varphi_\lambda \overline{\mathcal{J}} = 0 \]and \[ C \varphi_\lambda = \chi_\lambda(C) \varphi_\lambda \ \text{ for all } C \in \mathcal{Z} U_q(\mathfrak{g}). \]Clearly, we have \[ \langle \varphi_\lambda | \varphi_\mu \rangle = 0 \ \text{ if } \lambda \neq \mu. \]

Our problems can be stated as follows:
  • Find a systematic way to construct a coideal subalgebra $U_q^{\mathrm{tw}}(\mathfrak{k})$ of $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ for a given Gelfand pair $(\mathfrak{g}, \mathfrak{k})$.
  • Describe the structure of subalgebras $A_q(G/K)$ and $A_q(K \setminus G/K)$.
