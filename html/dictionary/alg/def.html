Definitions Concerning Algebras

Definition of Algebras

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
An algebra over $\mathbb{K}$ or a $\mathbb{K}$-algebra is a vector space $A$ over $\mathbb{K}$ equipped with a linear map \[ A \otimes A \rightarrow A;\ a \otimes b \mapsto ab, \]called the multiplication.

Definition of Associative Algebras

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $A$: a $\mathbb{K}$-algebra.
The algebra $A$ is said to be associative if it satisfies the associative law: \[ a \cdot (bc) = (ab) \cdot c \ \text{ for all } a,b,c \in A. \]

Definition of Unital Algebras

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $A$: a $\mathbb{K}$-algebra.
The algebra $A$ is said to be unital or to have a unit element if there exists $1 \in A$ such that \[ 1 \cdot a = a \cdot 1 = a \ \text{ for all } a \in A. \]
The linear homomorphism $\eta: \mathbb{K} \rightarrow A$ sending $1 \in \mathbb{K}$ to $1 \in A$ is called the unit.

Definition of Homomorphisms on Algebras

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $A, B$: two algebras over $\mathbb{K}$.
  • $f: A \rightarrow B$: a linear map.
The linear map $f$ is said to be an algebra homomorphism if \[ f(ab) = f(a) f(b)\ \text{ for all } a,b \in A. \]When both $A$ and $B$ are unital, it is further supposed to satisfy \[ f(1) = 1. \]
In terms of the diagrammatic definition, the linear map $f$ is an algebra homomorphism if and only if \[ f \circ m_A = m_B \circ (f \otimes f) \text{ and } f \circ \eta_A = \eta_B. \]

Opposite Algebra

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $A,$: an algebras over $\mathbb{K}$.
The opposite algebra is the algebra $A^\mathrm{op}$ whose underlying vector space is the same as $A$, and the multiplication is given by \[ a \cdot^\mathrm{op} b := ba \ \text{ for all } a,b \in A. \]
