Definitions Concerning Root Data

Definition of Root Data

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $a_{i,j} := \frac{2 i \cdot j}{i \cdot i}$ for each $i,j \in I$.
A root datum over $I$ is a quintuple $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$ consisting of
  • a free abelian group $X$ of finite rank, called the weight lattice,
  • a free abelian group $Y$ of finite rank, called the coroot lattice,
  • a perfect bilinear pairing $\langle , \rangle: Y \times X \rightarrow \mathbb{Z}$,
  • a subset $\Pi = \{ \alpha_i \mid i \in I \} \subset X$, called the set of simple roots,
  • a subset $\Pi^\vee = \{ h_i \mid i \in I \} \subset Y$, called the set of simple coroots,
such that \[ \langle h_i, \alpha_j \rangle = a_{i,j} \text{ for all } i,j \in I \]([Lus10, Section 2.2.1]).
Let $h : \mathbb{Z}[I] \rightarrow Y$ and $\alpha: \mathbb{Z}[I] \rightarrow X$ denote the linear maps defined as follows: \[ h(\nu) = \sum_{i \in I} \nu_i h_i, \quad \alpha(\nu) = \sum_{i \in I} \nu_i \alpha_i \ \text{ for all } \nu = \sum_{i \in I} \nu_i i \in \mathbb{Z}[I]. \]

Weyl Group Action

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: a root datum over $I$.
  • $W$: the Weyl group of $I$.
Then, $W$ acts on $Y$ and $X$ by \[ s_i(h) = h - \langle h, \alpha_i \rangle h_i, \quad s_i(\lambda) = \lambda - \langle h_i, \lambda \rangle \alpha_i, \quad \text{ for all } i \in I,\ h \in Y,\ \lambda \in X \]([Lus10, Section 2.2.6]).

Definition of Regular Root Data

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: a root datum over $I$.
The root datum $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$ is said to be
  • $X$-regular if $\Pi$ is linearly independent ([Lus10, Section 2.2.2]).
  • $Y$-regular if $\Pi^\vee$ is linearly independent ([Lus10, Section 2.2.2]).
  • regular if it is both $X$-regular and $Y$-regular.

Definition of Dominance Order

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: an $X$-regular root datum over $I$.
Then, there exists a partial ordering $\leq$ on $X$, called the dominance order, defined as follows. Let $\lambda,\mu \in X$. Then, we have $\lambda \leq \mu$ if and only if $\mu - \lambda \in \sum_{i \in I} \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0} \alpha_i$.

Definition of Dominant Weights

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: a $Y$-regular root datum over $I$.
A weight $\lambda \in X$ is said to be dominant if \[ \langle h_i, \lambda \rangle \geq 0 \text{ for all } i \in I \]([Lus10, Section 3.5.5]).
The set of dominant weights is denoted by $X^+$.

Properties

  • $\alpha_i = \alpha_j$ if and only if $i = j$.
  • $h_i = h_j$ if and only if $i = j$.
  • Root data over a Cartan datum of finite type are regular.
