Homomorphisms on Quantum Groups

Definition of the Anti-Algebra Involution $\sigma$

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: a root datum over $I$.
  • $\mathbf{U}$: the quantum group.
Then, there exists an anti-algebra involution $\sigma$ on $\mathbf{U}$ such that \[ \sigma(E_i) = E_i, \quad \sigma(F_i) = F_i, \quad \omega(K_h) = K_{-h},\ \text{ for all } i \in I \text{ and } h \in Y \]([Lus10, Section 3.1.3]).

Definition of the Bar-Involution

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: a root datum over $I$.
  • $\mathbf{U}$: the quantum group.
The bar-involution on $\mathbf{U}$ is the $\mathbb{Q}$-algebra automorphism $\overline{\cdot}$ such that \[ \overline{E_i} = E_i, \quad \overline{F_i} = F_i, \quad \overline{K_h} = K_{-h}, \quad \overline{q} = q^{-1}, \ \text{ for all } i \in I \text{ and } h \in Y \]([Lus10, Section 3.1.3]).

Definition of the Chevalley Involution

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: a root datum over $I$.
  • $\mathbf{U}$: the quantum group.
The Chevalley involution on $\mathbf{U}$ is the algebra automorphism $\omega$ such that \[ \omega(E_i) = F_i, \quad \omega(F_i) = E_i, \quad \omega(K_h) = K_{-h},\ \text{ for all } i \in I \text{ and } h \in Y \]([Lus10, Section 3.1.3]).

Definition of the Anti-Algebra Involution $\rho$

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: a root datum over $I$.
  • $\mathbf{U}$: the quantum group.
Then, there exists an anti-algebra involution $\rho$ on $\mathbf{U}$ such that \[ \rho(E_i) = q_i K_i F_i, \quad \rho(F_i) = q_i K_i^{-1} E_i, \quad \rho(K_h) = K_h,\ \text{ for all } i \in I \text{ and } h \in Y \]([Lus10, Section 19.1.1]).

Lusztig's Symmetries $T^?_{i,e}$

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $a_{i,j} := \frac{2 i \cdot j}{i \cdot i}$.
  • $d_i := \frac{i \cdot i}{2}$.
  • $q_i := q^{d_i}$.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: a root datum over $I$.
  • $\mathbf{U}$: the quantum group.
  • $i \in I$.
  • $e \in \{ 1, -1 \}$.
  • $? \in \{ \prime, \prime\prime \}$.
There exists a unique algebra automorphism $T^?_{i,e}$ on $\mathbf{U}$ satisfying the following: \begin{align*} \begin{split} &T'_{i,\pm}(E_j) = \begin{cases} -K_i^{\pm 1} F_i & \text{ if } j = i, \\ \sum_{r+s = -a_{i,j}} (-1)^r q_i^{\pm r} E_i^{(r)} E_j E_i^{(s)} & \text{ if } j \neq i, \end{cases} \\ &T'_{i,\pm}(F_j) = \begin{cases} -E_i K_i^{\mp 1} & \text{ if } j = i, \\ \sum_{r+s = -a_{i,j}} (-1)^r q_i^{\mp r} F_i^{(s)} F_j F_i^{(r)} & \text{ if } j \neq i, \end{cases} \\ &T'_{i,\pm}(K_h) = K_{s_i(h)}, \\ &T''_{i,\pm}(E_j) = \begin{cases} -F_i K_i^{\pm 1} & \text{ if } j = i, \\ \sum_{r+s = -a_{i,j}} (-1)^r q_i^{\mp r} E_i^{(s)} E_j E_i^{(r)} & \text{ if } j \neq i, \end{cases} \\ &T''_{i,\pm}(F_j) = \begin{cases} -K_i^{\mp 1} E_i & \text{ if } j = i, \\ \sum_{r+s = -a_{i,j}} (-1)^r q_i^{\pm r} F_i^{(r)} F_j F_i^{(s)} & \text{ if } j \neq i, \end{cases} \\ &T''_{i,\pm}(K_h) = K_{s_i(h)}. \end{split} \end{align*}For the definition of $s_i(h)$, see here.
