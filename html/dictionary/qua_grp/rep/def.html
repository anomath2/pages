Definitions Concerning Representations of Quantum Groups

Definition of Weight Modules

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: a root datum over $I$.
  • $\mathbf{U}$: the quantum group.
A $\mathbf{U}$-module $M$ is said to be a weight module if it admits a weight space decomposition \[ M = \bigoplus_{\lambda \in X} M_\lambda, \quad M_\lambda := \{ m \in M \mid K_h m = q^{\langle h, \lambda \rangle}m \text{ for all } h \in Y \}. \]The subspace $M_\lambda$ for $\lambda \in X$ is called the weight space of weight $\lambda$, and the vectors of $M_\lambda$ are called the weight vectors of weight $\lambda$.

Definition of Highest Weight Modules

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: a root datum over $I$.
  • $\mathbf{U}$: the quantum group.
A $\mathbf{U}$-module $M$ is said to be a highest weight module of highest weight $\lambda$ if there exists a nonzero vector $m \in M$, called a highest weight vector, satisfying the following.
  • $K_h m = q^{\langle h, \lambda \rangle} m$ for all $h \in Y$.
  • $E_i m = 0$ for all $i \in I$.
  • $\mathbf{U} m = M$.

Definition of Verma Modules

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: a root datum over $I$.
  • $\mathbf{U}$: the quantum group.
The Verma module of highest weight $\lambda$ is the $\mathbf{U}$-module $M(\lambda)$ satisfying the following universal property. Let $M$ be a highest weight $\mathbf{U}$-module of highest weight $\lambda$ with a highest weight vector $m$. Then, there exists a unique $\mathbf{U}$-module homomorphism $M(\lambda) \rightarrow M$ which sends $v_\lambda$ to $m$.

Definition of irreducible Highest Weight Modules

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: a root datum over $I$.
  • $\mathbf{U}$: the quantum group.
The irreducible highest weight module is the unique simple quotient $V(\lambda)$ of the Verma module $M(\lambda)$.

Definition of Integrable Modules

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $(Y, X, \langle , \rangle, \Pi^\vee, \Pi)$: a root datum over $I$.
  • $\mathbf{U}$: the quantum group.
A weight $\mathbf{U}$-module $M$ is said to be integrable if for each $m \in M$ and $i \in I$, there exists $N \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ such that \[ E_i^N m = F_i^N m = 0 \]([Lus10, Section 3.5.1]).
