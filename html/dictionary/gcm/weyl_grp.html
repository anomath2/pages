Weyl Group of a Generalized Cartan Matrix

Definition

Let
  • $A = (a_{i,j})_{i,j \in I}$: a GCM.
  • $(\mathfrak{h}, \Pi, \Pi^\vee)$: a realization of $A$.
The Weyl group $W$ of $A$ is the subgroup of $GL(\mathfrak{h}^*)$ generated by the elements $s_i$ for $i \in I$, called the fundamental reflections, given by \[ s_i(\lambda) := \lambda - \langle h_i, \lambda \rangle \alpha_i \text{ for all } \lambda \in \mathfrak{h}^*. \]([Kac90, Section 3.7])

Properties

  • The Weyl group $W$ is a Coxeter group with simple reflections $\{ s_i \mid i \in I \}$; the order of $s_i s_j$ for $i \neq j \in I$ is $2,3,4,6, \infty$ if $a_{i,j} a_{j,i} = 0,1,2,3,\geq 4$, respectively.
  • Let $J \subset I$. Then, the Weyl group of the generalized Cartan submatrix $(a_{i,j})_{i,j \in J}$ is the subgroup of $W$ generated by $\{ s_j \mid j \in J \}$.
