Properties of Bialgebras

Group-Like Elements Form a Semigroup

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $B$: a bialgebra over $\mathbb{K}$.
The set of group-like elements of $B$ form a semigroup with unit $1$.

Primitive Elements Form a Lie Algebra

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $B$: a bialgebra over $\mathbb{K}$.
The set of primitive elements of $B$ form a Lie algebra with Lie bracket being the commutator.

Necessary and Sufficient Condition for Being a Hopf Algebra

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $B$: a bialgebra over $\mathbb{K}$.
Then, the bialgebra $B$ is a Hopf algebra if and only if the algebra $\operatorname{Hom}_\mathbb{K}(B, B)$ with convolution product has the inverse of $\mathrm{id}_B$.
