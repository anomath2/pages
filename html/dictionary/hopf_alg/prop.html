Properties of Hopf Algebras

Antipode and Bialgebra Structure

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $H$: a Hopf algebra over $\mathbb{K}$.
Then, the antipode $S$ is an algebra anti-homomorphism on $H$, and is a coalgebra anti-homomorphism on $H$, i.e., \[ S(ab) = S(b)S(a), S(1) = 1, \Delta^\mathrm{op} \circ S = (S \otimes S) \circ \Delta, \epsilon \circ S = \epsilon. \]

Invertibility of Antipode

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $H$: a Hopf algebra over $\mathbb{K}$.
Then, the following are equivalent.
  • The antipode is invertible.
  • The bialgebra $H^\mathrm{op}$ is a Hopf algebra.
  • The bialgebra $H^\mathrm{cop}$ is a Hopf algebra.
In this case, the inverse $S^{-1}$ is the antipode of $H^\mathrm{op}$ and $H^\mathrm{cop}$.

Dual Hopf Algebra (Finite Dimension)

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $H$: a finite-dimensional Hopf algebra over $\mathbb{K}$.
Then, the dual vector space $H^*$ is equipped with the Hopf algebra structure as follows: \begin{align*} \begin{split} &(f \cdot g)(a) = \sum f(a_{(1)}) g(a_{(2)}), \\ &1_{H^*} = \epsilon, \\ &(\Delta(f))(a \otimes b) = f(ab), \\ &\epsilon_{H^*}(f) = f(1), \\ &(S_{H^*}(f))(a) = f(S(a)). \end{split} \end{align*}
Consequently, the canonical pairing \[ \langle , \rangle: H^* \times H \rightarrow \mathbb{K} \]is a nondegenerate dual pairing for the bialgebras $H^*$ and $H$.

Dual Pairing

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $G, H$: two Hopf algebras over $\mathbb{K}$.
  • $\langle , \rangle: G \times H \rightarrow \mathbb{K}$: a dual pairing as bialgebras.
Then, we have \[ \langle S(g), h \rangle = \langle g, S(h) \rangle \text{ for all } (g,h) \in G \times H. \]
