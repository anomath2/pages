Examples of Hopf Algebras

Algebra of $\mathbb{C}$-Valued Functions on a Finite Group

Let
  • $G$: a finite group.
  • $\mathcal{F}(G)$: the set of $\mathbb{C}$-valued functions on $G$.
Then, $\mathcal{F}(G)$ is a Hopf algebra such that \begin{align*} \begin{split} &(f \cdot f')(g) = f(g) f'(g), \\ &1(g) = 1, \\ &(\Delta(f))(g, g') = f(gg'), \\ &\epsilon(f) = f(e), \\ &(S(f))(g) = f(g^{-1}), \end{split} \end{align*}where we identified $\mathcal{F}(G \times G)$ with $\mathcal{F}(G) \otimes \mathcal{F}(G)$.

Universal Enveloping Algebra

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $\mathfrak{g}$: a Lie algebra over $\mathbb{K}$.
The universal enveloping algebra $U(\mathfrak{g})$ has a unique Hopf algebra structure such that \[ \Delta(x) = x \otimes 1 + 1 \otimes x,\ \epsilon(x) = 0,\ S(x) = -x \ \text{ for all } x \in \mathfrak{g}. \]
This Hopf algebra is cocommutative.

Representative Functions of a Compact Group

Let
  • $G$: a compact group.
  • $\mathcal{F}(G)$: the set of $\mathbb{C}$-valued functions on $G$.
  • $\mathcal{C}(T)$: the subspace of $\mathcal{F}(G)$ spanned by the matrix elements for a finite-dimensional continuous representation $T$ of $G$.
  • $\mathrm{Rep}(G)$: the subspace of $\mathcal{F}(G)$ spanned by $\mathcal{C}(T)$ for all finite-dimensional continuous representation of $G$.
Then, the subspace $\mathrm{Rep}(G)$ is a Hopf algebra of $\mathcal{F}(G)$, called the Hopf algebra of representative functions of $G$.
By the Peter-Weyl theorem, the subalgebra $\mathrm{Rep}(G)$ is dense in the algebra $C(G)$ of continuous functions on $G$.
Let $(t_{i,j})_{1 \leq i,j \leq n}$ be a matrix with $t_{i,j} \in \mathcal{F}(G)$. Then, the elements $t_{i,j}$ are matrix elements of some continuous finite-dimensional representation of $G$ if and only if they belong to $\mathrm{Rep}(G)$ and satisfy \[ \Delta(t_{i,j}) = \sum_{k=1}^n t_{i,k} \otimes t_{k,j}, \quad \epsilon(t_{i,j}) = \delta_{i,j}. \]This means that continuous finite-dimensional representations of $G$ can be defined completely in terms of $\mathrm{Rep}(G)$ without referring to $G$.

Coordinate Ring of a Matrix Group

Let
  • $G$: a matrix group $SL_N(\mathbb{C})$, $SO_N(\mathbb{C})$, or $Sp_N(\mathbb{C})$.
  • $\mathcal{F}(G)$: the set of $\mathbb{C}$-valued functions on $G$.
  • $u^i_j \in \mathcal{F}(G)$: the coordinate function, i.e., $u^i_j(g) = g_{i,j}$ if $g = (g_{i,j})_{1 \leq i,j \leq N}$.
  • $\mathcal{O}(G)$: the subalgebra of $\mathcal{F}(G)$ generated by $u^i_j$ with $1 \leq i,j \leq N$.
Then, the subalgebra $\mathcal{O}(G)$ is a Hopf algebra, called the coordinate ring of $G$.
We have \begin{align*} \begin{split} &\Delta(u^i_j) = \sum_k u^i_k \otimes u^k_j, \\ &\epsilon(u^i_j) = \delta_{i,j}. \end{split} \end{align*}
