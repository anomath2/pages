Properties of Coalgebras

Convolution Product

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $A$: a unital associative $\mathbb{K}$-algebra.
  • $C$: a $\mathbb{K}$-coalgebra.
Then, the vector space $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{K}}(C, A)$ becomes a unital associative algebra with multiplication being the convolution product and unit element $\epsilon \circ \eta$.
In particular (when $A = \mathbb{K}$), the dual vector space $C^*$ of the coalgebra $C$ is an algebra with multiplication \[ (f * g)(a) = ((f \otimes g) \circ \Delta)(a) = \sum f(a_{(1)}) g(a_{(2)}) \]and unit element $\epsilon$.
Note that the dual vector space of the algebra $A$ does not become a coalgebra in general since $(A \otimes A)^*$ is larger than $A^* \otimes A^*$ in general.
