Definitions Concerning Coalgebras

Definition of Coalgebras

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
A coalgebra over $\mathbb{K}$ or a $\mathbb{K}$-coalgebra is a vector space $C$ over $\mathbb{K}$ equipped with a linear map \[ \Delta: A \rightarrow A \otimes A, \]called the comultiplication and a linear map \[ \epsilon: A \rightarrow \mathbb{K}, \]called the counit, satisfying the coassociative law \[ (\Delta \otimes \mathrm{id}) \circ \Delta = (\mathrm{id} \otimes \Delta) \circ \Delta \]and \[ (\epsilon \otimes \mathrm{id}) \circ \Delta = \mathrm{id} = \mathrm{id} = (\mathrm{id} \otimes \epsilon) \circ \Delta. \]

Definition of Homomorphisms on Coalgebras

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $C, D$: two coalgebras over $\mathbb{K}$.
  • $f: C \rightarrow D$: a linear map.
The linear map $f$ is said to be a coalgebra homomorphism if \[ \Delta_D \circ f = (f \otimes f) \circ \Delta_A \text{ and } \epsilon_A = \epsilon_B \circ f. \]

Opposite Coalgebra

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $C,$: an algebras over $\mathbb{K}$.
The opposite coalgebra is the algebra $C^\mathrm{op}$ whose underlying vector space is the same as $C$, and the comultiplication is given by \[ \Delta^\mathrm{op} := \tau \circ \Delta, \]where $\tau$ denotes the flip operator. \[ \tau: A \otimes A \rightarrow A \otimes A;\ a \otimes b \mapsto b \otimes a. \]

Convolution Product

Let
  • $\mathbb{K}$: a field.
  • $A$: a $\mathbb{K}$-algebra.
  • $C$: a $\mathbb{K}$-coalgebra.
The convolution product on $\operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{K}}(C, A)$ is the linear map \[ * : \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{K}}(C, A) \otimes \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{K}}(C, A) \rightarrow \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{K}}(C, A) \]defined by \[ (f*g)(a) := (m \circ (f \otimes g) \circ \Delta)(a) = \sum f(a_{(1)}) g(a_{(2)}) \]for all $f,g \in \operatorname{Hom}_{\mathbb{K}}(C, A)$, $a \in A$.
