Properties of Lusztig Algebras

Presentation

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $\mathbf{f}$: the Lusztig algebra.
  • $\theta_i^{(n)} \in \mathbf{f}$: the divided power for $i \in I$ and $n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$.
The Lusztig algebra $\mathbf{f}$ is isomorphic to the $\mathbb{Q}(q)$-algebra with generators $\{ \theta_i \mid i \in I \}$ subject to the quantum Serre relations, i.e., \[ \sum_{r+s=1-a_{i,j}} (-1)^s \theta_i^{(r)} \theta_j \theta_i^{(s)} = 0 \quad \text{ for all } i \neq j \in I \]([Lus10, Theorem 33.1.3 (a)]).
