Skew-Derivations $r_i$ and ${}_ir$ on Lusztig Algebras

Definition

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $\mathbf{f}$: the Lusztig algebra.
  • $i \in I$.
There exists unique linear endomorphisms $r_i$ and ${}_ir$, called the right and left skew derivations, respectively, on $\mathbf{f}$ satisfying the following ([Lus10, Section 1.2.13]).
  • $r_i(1) = 0$.
  • $r_i(\theta_j) = \delta_{i,j}$.
  • $r_i(xy) = q^{i \cdot |y|} r_i(x)y + x r_i(y)$ for all homogeneous vectors in $x,y \in \mathbf{f}$.
  • ${}_ir(1) = 0$.
  • ${}_ir(\theta_j) = \delta_{i,j}$.
  • ${}_ir(xy) = {}_ir(x)y + q^{|x| \cdot i} x {}_ir(y)$ for all homogeneous vectors in $x,y \in \mathbf{f}$.

Properties

  • If $x \in \mathbf{f}_\nu$ with $\nu = \sum_{i \in I} \nu_i i \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$, then $r_i(x), {}_ir(x) \in \mathbf{f}_{\nu-i}$. In particular, $r_i(x) = {}_ir(x) = 0$ if $\nu_i = 0$.
  • If $x \in \mathbf{f}_\nu$, $\nu \neq 0$, and $r_i(x) = 0$ for all $i \in I$, then $x = 0$ ([Lus10, Lemma 1.2.15]).
