Definitions Concerning Lusztig Algebras

Abstract

The Lusztig algebra is a $\mathbb{Q}(q)$-algebra associated with a Cartan datum. It is constructed by nondegenerating the pre-Lusztig algebra associated with the same Cartan datum.

Definition of Pre-Lusztig Algebras

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $d_i := \frac{i \cdot i}{2}$ for all $i \in I$.
  • $q$: an indeterminate.
  • $q_i := q^{d_i}$ for all $i \in I$.
The pre-Lusztig algebra is the unital free associative $\mathbb{Q}(q)$-algebra ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ with generators \[ \{ \theta_i \mid i \in I \}, \]equipped with a symmetric bilinear form $(,)$, called the standard form, defined as follows.
For each $\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$, set \[ {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}_\nu := \operatorname{Span}_{\mathbb{Q}(q)} \left\{ \theta_{i_1} \cdots \theta_{i_r} \mid i_1,\dots,i_r \in I, \sum_{k=1}^r i_k = \nu \right\}. \]Then, we have \[ {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f} = \bigoplus_{\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]} {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}_\nu \quad \text{ as vector spaces.} \]This makes ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ into a $\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$-graded algebra. For each $x \in {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}_\nu$, let $|x|$ denote $\nu$.
The tensor square ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}^{\otimes 2}$ admits a $\mathbb{Q}(q)$-algebra structure \[ (x_1 \otimes x_2) \cdot (y_1 \otimes y_2) := q^{|x_2| \cdot |y_1|} x_1y_1 \otimes x_2y_2 \]for homogeneous elements $x_1,x_2,y_1,y_2 \in {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$.
Similarly, the tensor cube ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}^{\otimes 3}$ admits a $\mathbb{Q}(q)$-algebra structure: \[ (x_1 \otimes x_2 \otimes x_3) \cdot (y_1 \otimes y_2 \otimes y_3) := q^{|x_3| \cdot (|y_1| + |y_2|) + |x_2| \cdot |y_1|} x_1y_1 \otimes x_2y_2 \otimes x_3y_3 \]for homogeneous elements $x_1,x_2,x_3,y_1,y_2,y_3 \in {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$.
Since ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ is a free algebra, there exists a unique algebra homomorphism \[ r: {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f} \rightarrow {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}^{\otimes 2} \]such that \[ r(\theta_i) = \theta_i \otimes 1 + 1 \otimes \theta_i. \]It is called the comultiplication map on ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$.
Now, the standard form on ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ is defined to be the unique symmetric bilinear form satisfying the following.
  • $(1,1) = 1$.
  • $(\theta_i, \theta_j) = \delta_{i,j} \frac{1}{1-q_i^{-2}}$ for all $i,j \in I$.
  • $(x, y_1y_2) = (r(x), y_1 \otimes y_2)$ for all $x,y_1,y_2 \in {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$, where \[ (x_1 \otimes x_2, y_1 \otimes y_2) := (x_1,y_1) \cdot (x_2,y_2) \text{ for all } x_1,x_2,y_1,y_2 \in {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}. \]
The existence of such a form follows from [Lus10, Proposition 1.2.3].

Definition of Lusztig Algebras

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$: the pre-Lusztig algebra.
  • $\mathcal{I}$: the radical of the standard form on ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$.
The Lusztig algebra is the quotient algebra $\mathbf{f} := {}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}/\mathcal{I}$ ([Lus10, Section 1.2.5]). The images of generators $\theta_i$ for $i \in I$ of ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ are still denoted by $\theta_i$.
The bilinear form on $\mathbf{f}$ induced from the standard form on ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}$ is called the standard form on $\mathbf{f}$.

Definition of Divided Powers

Let
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $d_i := \frac{i \cdot i}{2}$ for each $i \in I$.
  • $q$: an indeterminate.
  • $q_i := q^{\frac{i \cdot i}{2}}$ for eahc $i \in I$.
  • $\mathbf{f}$: the Lusztig algebra.
  • $[n]_i!$: the quantum factorial for $n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ in variable $q_i$ for each $i \in I$.
The vectors of the form \[ \theta_i^{(n)} := \frac{\theta_i^n}{[n]_{q_i}!} \]with $i \in I$ and $n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ are called the divided powers.

Integral Form of Lusztig Algebras

Let
  • $\mathcal{A} := \mathbb{Z}[q,q^{-1}]$.
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $q$: an indeterminate.
  • $\theta_i^{(n)}$: the divided power for $i \in I$ and $n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$.
The integral form of $\mathbf{f}$ is the $\mathcal{A}$-subalgebra ${}_\mathcal{A}\mathbf{f}$ generated by the divided powers \[ \{ \theta_i^{(n)} \mid i \in I, n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0} \} \]([Lus10, Section 1.4.7]).

Properties

  • $\mathbf{f}$ is generated by $\theta_i$ for $i \in I$. A set of defining relations with respect to these generators is known (see here).
  • $\mathbf{f}$ is $\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$-graded i.e., \[ \mathbf{f} = \bigoplus_{\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]} \mathbf{f}_\nu, \] where \[ \mathbf{f}_\nu := \operatorname{Span}_{\mathbb{Q}(q)} \left\{ \theta_{i_1} \cdots \theta_{i_r} \mid i_1,\dots,i_r \in I, \sum_{k=1}^r i_k = \nu \right\}. \] For $x \in \mathbf{f}_\nu$, the $|x|$ denotes $\nu$.
  • $\mathbf{f}$ is $\mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$-graded i,e., \[ \mathbf{f} = \bigoplus_{n \geq 0} \mathbf{f}_n, \quad \mathbf{f}_n := \sum_{\substack{\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I] \\ \operatorname{tr} \nu = n}} \mathbf{f}_\nu, \] where $\operatorname{tr} \sum_{i \in I} \nu_i i := \sum_{i \in I} \nu_i$.
  • The standard form on $\mathbf{f}$ is nondegenerate.
  • The tensor square $\mathbf{f}^{\otimes 2}$ and the tensor cube $\mathbf{f}^{\otimes 3}$ admit associative algebra structure induced from that of ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}^{\otimes 2}$ and ${}^{\prime} \mathbf{f}^{\otimes 3}$, respectively.

Memo

  • The Lusztig algebra $\mathbf{f}$ can be constructed geometrically, which leads to a canonical basis via the theory of perverse sheaves.
  • The Lusztig algebra $\mathbf{f}$ is isomorphic to the negative/positive part of the quantized enveloping algebra.
  • The base field can be changed to an arbitrary ring (with a unit element) via the integral form.
