Canonical Bases of Lusztig Algebras

Definition of the Canonical Signed Basis

Let
  • $\mathcal{A} := \mathbb{Z}[q,q^{-1}]$.
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $\mathbf{f}$: the Lusztig algebra.
  • $\overline{\cdot}$: the bar-involution on $\mathbf{f}$.
  • ${}_\mathcal{A} \mathbf{f}$: the integral form of $\mathbf{f}$.
The canonical signed basis of $\mathbf{f}$ is the subset $\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}$ given by \[ \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}} := \{ x \in {}_\mathcal{A}\mathbf{f} \mid \overline{x} = x,\ (x,x) \in 1 + q^{-1} \mathbb{Z}[\![q^{-1}]\!] \}. \]

Properties of the Signed Canonical Basis

  • $\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}$ is a signed basis of ${}_\mathcal{A}\mathbf{f}$ ([Lus10, Theorem 14.2.3 (a)]).
  • If $b \neq \pm b' \in \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}$, then $(b, b') \in q^{-1} \mathbb{Z}[\![q^{-1}]\!]$ ([Lus10, Theorem 14.2.3 (b)]).
  • $\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_\nu := \mathbf{f}_\nu \cap \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}$ is a signed basis of $\mathbf{f}_\nu$ for all $\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$.
  • $\theta_i^{(n)} \in \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}$ for all $i \in I$, $n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ ([Lus10, Section 14.2.5]).
  • $\sigma(\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}) = \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}$, where $\sigma$ denotes an anti-algebra involution ([Lus10, Section 14.2.5]).

Definition of the Kashiwara Operators

Let
  • $\mathcal{A} := \mathbb{Z}[q,q^{-1}]$.
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $\mathbf{f}$: the Lusztig algebra.
  • $\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}$: the canonical signed basis.
  • $i \in I$.
  • $n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$.
  • $\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; \geq n} := \theta_i^n \mathbf{f} \cap \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}$.
  • $\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; n} := \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; \geq n} \setminus \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; \geq n+1}$.
There exists a bijection \[ \pi_{i; n} : \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; 0} \rightarrow \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; n} \]sending $b \in \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; 0}$ to a unique $b' \in \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i;n}$ such that \[ \theta_i^{(n)} b \in b' + \mathcal{A} \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; \geq n+1} \]([Lus10, Section 14.3]).
We call the bijections $\pi_{i;n}$ for $i \in I$ and $n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}$ the Kashiwara operators on $\mathbf{f}$.

Definition of the Canonical Basis

Let
  • $\mathcal{A} := \mathbb{Z}[q,q^{-1}]$.
  • $I$: a Cartan datum.
  • $\mathbf{f}$: the Lusztig algebra.
  • $\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}$: the canonical signed basis of $\mathbf{f}$.
  • $\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; \geq n} := \theta_i^n \mathbf{f} \cap \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}$.
  • $\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; n} := \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; \geq n} \setminus \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; \geq n+1}$.
  • $\pi_{i;n}: \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; 0} \rightarrow \mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; n}$: the Kashiwara operator on $\mathbf{f}$.
The canonical basis of $\mathbf{f}$ is the subset $\mathbf{B}$ defined by \[ \mathbf{B} := \bigsqcup_{\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]} \mathbf{B}_\nu, \quad \mathbf{B}_\nu := \bigcup_{\substack{i \in I \\ 0 < n \leq \nu_i}} \pi_{i; n}(\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}}_{i; 0} \cap \mathbf{B}_{\nu - n \cdot i}) \quad (\nu = \sum_{i \in I} \nu_i \cdot i). \]

Properties of the Canonical Basis

  • $\mathbf{B}^{\mathrm{sgn}} = \mathbf{B} \sqcup (- \mathbf{B})$ ([Lus10, Section 14.4.2]).
  • $\mathbf{B}$ is a basis of ${}_{\mathcal{A}(\mathbb{Z})}\mathbf{f}$ ([Lus10, Theorem 14.4.3 (d)]).
  • $\mathbf{B}_\nu$ is a basis of ${}_{\mathcal{A}(\mathbb{Z})}\mathbf{f}_\nu$ for all $\nu \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}[I]$ ([Lus10, Theorem 14.4.3 (e)]).
